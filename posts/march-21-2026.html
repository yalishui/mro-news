Supply Chain Developments

CFM56 Teardown Capacity Expansion

  • Jet AirWerks + Stratton Aviation: Strategic partnership announced March 18, 2026 for CFM56-5B/7B teardowns at Jet AirWerks' Engine Services Center in Arkansas City, Kansas
  • Investment: Jet AirWerks invested in gantry crane infrastructure and dedicated engine induction stations
  • Capacity: Dedicated teardown capacity to support Stratton Aviation's planned 2026 expansion of CFM56 engine asset portfolio
  • Turnaround: Ability to convert whole engine assets to usable inventory within weeks
  • Market Impact: Addresses ongoing USM (Used Serviceable Material) supply constraints; improves aftermarket efficiency

Source: MRO Business Today, March 20, 2026; PRNewswire, March 18, 2026

Engine Supply Chain Constraints Continue

  • Airbus-Pratt & Whitney talks reveal GTF and LEAP engine availability as primary A320neo bottleneck

ATEQ Aviation: Strategic Acquisition

  • March 20, 2026: ATEQ Aviation announced acquisition of T-RX avionics testing solution (formerly owned by CCX Technologies)
  • Integration: Combines ATEQ's ADSE series air data test sets with T-RX advanced avionics diagnostic platform
  • Regulatory Compliance: Enables MROs to perform 14 CFR 91.411 and 91.413 required tests (pitot-static, transponder certifications)
  • Operational Benefits:
  • Synchronized testing of full static systems and avionics
  • 60% reduction in aircraft downtime potential (robotic painting reference)
  • Reduced equipment footprint in hangars
  • Streamlined workflow from single vendor
