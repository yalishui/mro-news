Market Updates and Growth Trends

Jet Fuel Crisis: Sharpest Shock in Over a Decade

  • Trigger: Late-February 2026 Middle East conflict escalation + Strait of Hormuz supply disruptions
  • February 2026 average U.S. airline jet fuel cost: $2.39/gallon (BTS)
  • Mid-April 2026 composite spot prices: >$4.78/gallon (BTS, S&P Global Platts, IATA)
  • Mid-April 2026 retail FBO prices: >$8.60/gallon
  • American Airlines Q1 warning (April 23): Spike will add >$4 billion to its 2026 fuel bill if prices hold
  • Delta Air Lines Q1: ~$2 billion incremental fuel cost hit in Q2 2026 alone
  • United Airlines: CEO Scott Kirby — fares need to rise 15–20% to offset; 5 fare increases implemented in 2026; checked-bag fees raised $10

MRO Demand Recalibration Under Fuel Shock

  • 2026 global MRO demand base-case trim: 4–7% (line maintenance and legacy-engine shop visits see steepest near-term contraction)
  • 2025 global MRO market: $136B (+8% YoY) — Oliver Wyman
  • 2030 projection: ~$193B — Oliver Wyman
  • Winning MRO segments: Condition-based maintenance, predictive maintenance, engine performance restoration, heavy checks pulled forward, SAF compatibility testing
  • Losing MRO segments: Line maintenance (lower utilization), routine engine shop visits, legacy-engine shop visits

Regional Expansion Activities

Icelandair Plans Vilnius Technical Services Center (April 30)

  • Icelandair plans to establish a technical services center in Vilnius, Lithuania in May 2026
  • Hiring: 15 specialists in the first year
  • Roles: Maintenance coordinators, technical records specialists, planning engineers, CAMO engineers
  • Source: Invest Lithuania (national investment promotion agency)
  • Significance: Icelandair expanding third-party technical services into Eastern Europe; tapping Lithuania's growing aviation talent pool

Setna iO Acquires 737-800 and V2500 for Teardown (April 30)

  • Setna iO acquired a Boeing 737-800 (previously operated by Air Europa) and an IAE V2500 engine for teardown
  • 737-800 teardown location: ecube in Castellon, Spain
  • V2500 teardown location: Not yet disclosed
  • Significance: Growing teardown/recycling supply chain; used serviceable material (USM) market expanding as operators seek cost relief from new parts shortages

Supply Chain Developments

Airline Capacity Discipline Accelerates MRO Shifts

  • United Airlines: Cutting summer 2026 flight volume by 5%
  • Lufthansa Group: Removing 20,000 short-haul flights through October 2026 (saves ~40,000 metric tons of jet fuel); grounding 27 CityLine aircraft earlier than planned
  • SAS: Cancelled ~1,000 flights in April 2026
  • European carriers: Consolidating routes onto larger aircraft; reducing utilization by 6–9% at major hubs
  • JetBlue: Describes environment as "biggest headwind since the pandemic"; has not ruled out seeking government support

Fleet Retirement Acceleration — MRO Market Dual Edge

  • Airlines accelerating retirement of pre-2015 narrowbody aircraft (737-800s, A320ceos) which burn 15–22% more fuel per seat than newer models
  • Lessor data: 25–30% increase in early-return negotiations since March 2026
  • MRO impact: Lower utilization reduces line-maintenance events; BUT heavy maintenance (C-checks, D-checks) for efficient "keep" fleet and early-returned aircraft is rising

Technology and Investment News

Engine Performance Restoration Prioritized

  • CFM56 and V2500 engine washes being advanced by 300–500 flight hours (previously discretionary, now economically urgent)
  • Fuel-saving modifications installed during accelerated downtime: engine performance restoration, drag-reduction kits, deep engine washes
  • Each 1% improvement in specific fuel consumption = millions of dollars in savings at current price levels

SAF Compatibility Testing Accelerates

  • Airlines have brought forward SAF blend trials and engine compatibility checks by 6–12 months
  • Viewed as hedge against future carbon pricing and long-term fuel cost mitigation
  • MRO providers incorporating SAF compatibility checks into heavy maintenance scopes

Private Equity: Selective Premium Valuations

  • Premium valuations: Platforms with predictive-maintenance capabilities and next-generation engine exposure (GTF, LEAP, XWB) attracting premium valuations
  • Pressure on legacy-exposed providers: Pure-play heavy-maintenance providers heavily exposed to legacy narrowbody fleets face volume pressure; pivoting to efficiency-upgrade packages

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Jet Fuel — MRO as Strategic Efficiency Lever

  • Fuel as % of operating expenses: Jumped from 25–30% historical norm to dominant expense line
  • Airline actions: Capacity cuts, fleet retirement acceleration, maintenance pulled forward for efficient aircraft
  • MRO solution: Heavy checks pulled forward 3–6 months for efficient fleet; fuel-saving modifications installed during planned downtime; engine washes accelerated

Challenge 3: Hedging Renaissance — Possible Stabilization

  • Several U.S. majors have signaled possible limited fuel-hedging programs for 2027, which would stabilize cost visibility and slow aggressive capacity/maintenance cuts
  • Next 90 days critical: De-escalation or increased non-OPEC supply could moderate prices by $0.80–1.20/gallon by Q3 2026
