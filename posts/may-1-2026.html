Market Updates and Growth Trends

Jet Fuel Crisis: May 1, 2026 Status

  • Mid-April 2026 composite spot prices: >$4.78/gallon (BTS, S&P Global Platts, IATA)
  • Retail FBO prices mid-April: >$8.60/gallon
  • Q3 2026 price moderation scenario: $0.80–1.20/gallon drop possible if supply improves (Boston Warwick projection)
  • American Airlines Q1 warning (April 23): Spike will add >$4 billion to 2026 fuel bill
  • Delta Air Lines Q1: ~$2 billion incremental fuel cost hit in Q2 2026 alone
  • Fleet rationalization accelerating: Airlines retiring pre-2015 narrowbodies (15–22% higher fuel burn per seat); replacement with 737 MAX, A320neo family

MRO Demand Bifurcation — May 2026

  • 2025 global MRO market: $136B (+8% YoY) — Oliver Wyman
  • 2030 projection: ~$193B — Oliver Wyman
  • 2026 demand trim from base case: 4–7% (line maintenance and legacy-engine shop visits see steepest contraction)
  • Rising segments: Heavy checks pulled forward (fuel-saving mods), engine performance restoration, SAF compatibility testing, digital-twin predictive maintenance

Regional Expansion Activities

Executive Jet Support (EJS): A330 Acquisition + ATR72-500 Teardown (May 1)

  • EJS signed a letter of intent (LoI) to acquire an Airbus A330 from Air Caraïbes Atlantique
  • Additional acquisition: ATR72-500 from FlyCAA
  • ATR72-500 disposition: Will be torn down in Poland to support EJS's continued investment in the ATR program
  • Significance: EJS (executive/business jet support provider) expanding into commercial aircraft teardown and USM (used serviceable material) harvesting; ATR program expansion signals confidence in turboprop aftermarket demand

APOC Aviation: "Zero-Cycles" A321 Landing Gear + Asset Tripling (May 1)

  • APOC Aviation acquired a "zero-cycles-since-new" Safran landing gear for the Airbus A321 (compatible with Airbus' new engine option / NEO fleet)
  • Asset growth: APOC has tripled its landing gear assets in the last 12 months
  • Portfolio expansion: Added options for both widebody and narrowbody platforms
  • Target market: Both new and aging aircraft platforms
  • Significance: Independent USM providers scaling inventory to meet operator demand for cost-effective alternatives to new OEM parts; landing gear shortage (especially for A320/A321 NEO) driving aftermarket investment

Supply Chain Developments

Lessor Early-Return Surge Persists

  • 25–30% increase in early-return negotiations for leased aircraft since March 2026
  • Early returns include pre-agreed heavy-check packages partly funded by lessees to speed re-marketing
  • MRO revenue impact: Short-term boost from heavy-check packages for early-returned aircraft; compressed long-term visibility for legacy narrowbody fleets

Engine MRO Capacity Constraints Continue

  • GE Aerospace Q1 2026: Warning of "lower volume related to shop visits, spare parts and spare engines" but noting rising demand for performance-restoration modules
  • Magnetic Engines: South African Civil Aviation Authority approval (April 28) — one of limited MROs with this regulatory approval; positions Magnetic for broader Africa/EMEA market access

Technology and Investment News

Predictive Maintenance & Digital Twin Adoption (May 2026)

  • Lufthansa Technik: 15–20% increase in bookings for digital-twin-enabled predictive programs
  • MTU Aero Engines: 15–20% increase in bookings for digital-twin-enabled predictive programs
  • Technology drivers: Each 1% improvement in specific fuel consumption = millions in savings at current fuel price levels
  • Engine wash acceleration: CFM56 and V2500 engine washes advanced by 300–500 flight hours

SAF Compatibility Testing Accelerates

  • Airlines have brought forward SAF blend trials and engine compatibility checks by 6–12 months
  • Viewed as hedge against future carbon pricing and long-term fuel cost mitigation
  • MRO providers incorporating SAF compatibility checks into heavy maintenance scopes

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Jet Fuel — MRO as Cost-Mitigation Tool

  • Fuel share of operating costs: Jumped from 25–30% historical norm to dominant expense line
  • Airline actions: Capacity cuts (United –5% summer), route consolidation onto larger aircraft (6–9% utilization reduction), fleet retirement acceleration
  • MRO solution: Heavy checks pulled forward 3–6 months for efficient "keep" fleet; fuel-saving modifications (engine performance restoration, drag-reduction kits, deep engine washes) installed during planned downtime

Challenge 2: Next 90 Days Critical for MRO Sector

  • Variable 1: Hormuz and supply dynamics — de-escalation or increased non-OPEC crude could moderate prices by $0.80–1.20/gallon by Q3 2026
  • Variable 2: Demand elasticity — summer leisure travel resilient so far, but 15–20% effective fare increase may soften load factors, further pressuring utilization and line-maintenance volumes
  • Variable 3: Hedging renaissance — several U.S. majors signaled possible limited fuel-hedging programs for 2027, which would stabilize cost visibility and slow aggressive capacity/maintenance cuts
