Market Updates and Growth Trends

IBA: Engine Shortages and MRO Backlog Keep Values and Lease Rates Elevated

  • IBA (aviation market intelligence and advisory firm) published its latest engine value report on March 24, 2026, confirming that persistent MRO backlogs and supply chain constraints continue to keep spare engine values and lease rates at elevated levels (IBA Aero, March 24)
  • Narrowbody engine trends:
  • CFM56-5B and CFM56-7B: Values have plateaued after ~18 months of growth — may be reaching market ceiling
  • V2500-A5: Still commanding $11M–$13M per transaction due to sustained demand for available units
  • LEAP and PW1100G (GTF): Ongoing on-wing time challenges keeping AOG levels elevated and spare engine demand persistent
  • Widebody engine trends:
  • Boeing 777-300ER engines: Full-life values have risen significantly since 2023, now exceeding $32 million; spot lease rates at $170,000–$185,000/month
  • A330ceo / Trent 700: Post-overhaul values climbing with market trend
  • Regional/turboprop engines:
  • CF34-8E: Half-life value slightly down due to maintenance-related adjustments, not demand weakness
  • PW127M: Supply chain limitations (critical parts shortages) creating upward pressure on values
  • IBA outlook: MRO demand, supply chain constraints, and delayed aircraft production to remain key drivers of engine values in near term; demand continues to outpace supply across most segments

Source: IBA Aero, March 24, 2026

FL Technics Outpaces Global MRO Sector with 16% CAGR

  • FL Technics (Avia Solutions Group subsidiary) is growing at nearly 6x the global MRO market average, with revenue climbing from $105.3M (2016) to $405.2M (2025) — a 16% CAGR versus the industry's 2.7% average (The Silicon Review, March 24)
  • 2026 revenue target: $680M; 2030 target: $1 billion
  • Customer growth: 792 (2016) → 1,433 (2025) — over 80% increase
  • Market share: Doubled from 0.2% to 0.4% globally (2017–2025)
  • Growth strategy: Focus on underserved emerging markets in Asia, EMEA, and Americas while larger MROs compete in saturated markets; combination of organic growth + selective M&A (notably JOB AIR Technic acquisition)
  • Punta Cana mega-facility (Dominican Republic):
  • $70 million strategic investment
  • Phase 1: 20,000 sqm hangar with 5 C-check bays; total infrastructure 52,000 sqm
  • Phases 2 & 3: Expansion to 12, then 20 bays — targeting largest MRO facility in Latin America
  • Focus aircraft: A320 family and Boeing 737NG/MAX
  • Positions FL Technics to serve North, Central, and South American carriers

Source: The Silicon Review, March 24, 2026

Regional Expansion Activities

Aerotec Acquires Croatia-Based Global Aerotech — European MRO Consolidation Continues

  • Aerotec Europe GmbH announced on March 24, 2026 the acquisition of Global Aerotech d.o.o, a Croatian MRO provider specializing in base maintenance, line maintenance, and component repair (AviTrader, March 24)
  • Financial terms: Not disclosed
  • Strategic context: Acquisition is part of Aerotec's post-2022 growth strategy under Borromin Capital Fund IV ownership — building an integrated, digitalized MRO and aviation aftermarket platform
  • Recent acquisition history:
  • June 2024: aviationscouts (aircraft interior aftermarket — seat refurbishment)
  • July 2025: Opremic trade GmbH (IT firm — MRO documentation and process digitalization)
  • November 2025: Dubai component repair shop (cabin interior MRO, international footprint)
  • March 2026: Global Aerotech (base/line maintenance + component repair, European expansion)
  • Strategic impact: Adds technical capabilities in base maintenance and line maintenance; strengthens Aerotec's European geographic coverage; advances integrated digital MRO platform buildout

Source: AviTrader, March 24, 2026

Akasa Air to Launch First MRO Facility at Noida International Airport

  • Akasa Air (India's fastest-growing airline) confirmed plans to establish its first MRO facility at the upcoming Noida International Airport (Jewar), Uttar Pradesh (Aviation Week, March 24)
  • The facility will provide a wide range of aircraft maintenance services upon the new airport's opening
  • Adds to the rapidly expanding India MRO investment wave: Safran (Hyderabad €200M), IndiGo (Bangalore), GMR Aero Technic, and now Akasa Air (Noida)
  • India's domestic fleet is on track to nearly double from 700+ to 1,300+ narrowbodies by 2030 — driving self-sufficiency MRO investment

Source: Aviation Week Network, March 24, 2026

Aviaheads Receives Part-CAMO Approval from Estonian Civil Aviation Authority

  • Aviaheads received regulatory approval as an independent Continuing Airworthiness Management Organisation (CAMO) from the Estonian Transport Administration (Aviation Week, March 24)
  • Scope of approval: Airbus A320 Classic series, A330, and Boeing 737; includes Airworthiness Review Certificates
  • Reflects the growing market for independent CAMO services as airlines separate maintenance management from execution

Source: Aviation Week Network, March 24, 2026

Supply Chain Developments

Aviation Supply Chain: Structural Constraints Persisting Into 2026

  • Key supply chain metrics as of March 2026 (Safe Fly Aviation / industry composite):
  • Aircraft order backlog: >17,000 aircraft (Boeing + Airbus combined; years to clear at current rates)
  • Cumulative delivery shortfall: >5,300 aircraft ordered but not delivered on time
  • 2025 supply chain cost impact: >$11 billion (delayed deliveries, maintenance cost escalation, inventory buildup)
  • Part lead times: Critical parts (castings, forgings, bearings, avionics LRUs) now 20–40+ weeks, up from 4–6 weeks historically
  • Engine MRO share: ~42% of total 2026 MRO spend — remains single largest sub-segment
  • Root causes: GTF powder-metal disc inspections still consuming shop capacity; LEAP engines entering first-shop-visit cycle; certified technician shortfall continues after 2020–21 industry attrition

Industry Outlook

March 24 reinforces two dominant MRO structural themes:

1. Engine values and lease rates remain firmly elevated. IBA's report confirms the MRO backlog continues to suppress spare engine availability, keeping values and lease rates high across narrowbody, widebody, and regional platforms. While some CFM56 types show plateau signs, LEAP and GTF segments remain under acute pressure — and replacement demand from delayed aircraft deliveries continues to fill the pipeline.

2. Geographic MRO capacity investment is accelerating in every region. In a single day: FL Technics committed $70M to Latin America's largest planned MRO facility; GE Aerospace committed €110M+ to European engine MRO; Lufthansa Technik opened a new hangar in Dubai; and Akasa Air confirmed India's Noida airport will host its first MRO base. This synchronized global capex wave reflects the industry's conviction that MRO demand will remain structurally elevated through the decade.
