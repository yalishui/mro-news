MRO Greater China 2026 Officially Announced — Beijing, May 26–28

  • The inaugural MRO Greater China (大中华区MRO及航材供应链展) expo was officially announced this week, to be held May 26–28, 2026 at the Beijing Capital International Exhibition Centre (Hall B1) (Huanqiu / Civil Aviation China, April 2–4)
  • Scale: Near 20,000 sqm of exhibition floor; 200+ solution providers confirmed; projected 16,000+ professional attendees
  • Confirmed exhibitors: Boeing, Airbus, AMECO (Beijing), Haeco, Liebherr-Aerospace, ST Engineering (Singapore), ACMRO (Sichuan International MRO), COMAC, Air China, China Southern, China Eastern, Hainan Airlines, East Airlines Technics, and 120+ international and domestic firms
  • Organiser: Co-hosted by China Aviation Supplies Holding Company (CASC), Informa (Aviation Week), and 英富曼企业管理上海 (Informa China); professionally guided by the Civil Aviation Maintenance Association of China (CAMAC)
  • Conference track: Parallel "2026 Aviation Maintenance & Aviation Materials Supply Chain Summit" with theme "Linking the Globe · Building Safety Together"
  • Why it matters: China's domestic MRO market is the fastest-growing in the Asia-Pacific. MRO Greater China marks a major step toward China becoming not just the world's largest aviation market by volume, but also a global MRO hub in its own right — reducing reliance on overseas overhaul centres

LEVEL Suspends Barcelona–San Francisco Flights Citing Engine Shortage

  • LEVEL (Vueling Group / IAG low-cost long-haul brand) has suspended its Barcelona–San Francisco route due to an engine shortage, becoming one of the first public route suspension attributions directly to the MRO capacity crisis in 2026 (Aerospace Global News, April 4)
  • The aircraft type affected is understood to be A330 equipped with Rolls-Royce Trent 700 engines, where shop visit backlogs continue to extend lease rates and reduce available flying aircraft
  • This is the latest in a series of European long-haul capacity disruptions caused by engine supply issues that have cascaded from GTF grounding requirements onto the broader widebody MRO ecosystem
  • Industry context: Engine OEMs and independent MRO providers have all cited 2026 as the year engine shop visit demand reaches its highest point — with wait times stretching 12–18 months for Trent 700 and 6–12 months for CFM56-7B
  • Why it matters: Route suspensions due to maintenance capacity (rather than commercial demand) are a new phenomenon — a direct signal that the MRO supply-demand imbalance is now operationally visible to the travelling public, not just financial analysts

Regulatory & Technology

FAA Sets Certification Pathway for ZeroAvia Megawatt-Class Electric Engine

  • ZeroAvia has achieved a key milestone as the FAA formally established a certification pathway for its megawatt-class hydrogen-electric propulsion system (MRO Business Today, April 4)
  • The FAA's Issue Paper covers ZeroAvia's ZA2000-RJ engine, targeting a 50-seat commuter aircraft application
  • The certification pathway is expected to take approximately 5–7 years, targeting first commercial flights in the early 2030s
  • MRO implications: Hydrogen-electric propulsion will require entirely new MRO frameworks — no turbine hot section, no fuel system as currently defined, new hydrogen storage inspection and servicing protocols
  • Aviation Week Network flagged the ZeroAvia milestone in its April 6 preview as a harbinger of MRO transformation: "The workforce that will service these aircraft doesn't fully exist yet"
  • Why it matters: FAA setting a certification path signals regulatory intent; MRO operators and training organisations need to begin workforce preparation now, as the skills gap for electric/hydrogen maintenance will be larger than the current tech gap for composite airframes

Facility & Network Expansions

Cutter Aviation Opens Fifth Location — Colorado Springs, Expanding Southwest Network

  • Cutter Aviation, a full-service FBO and MRO provider, formally opened its fifth location in Colorado Springs, Colorado, expanding its Southwest US service network (MRO Business Today, April 4)
  • The Colorado Springs facility offers aircraft maintenance, avionics services, and line maintenance for business aviation and general aviation customers
  • Cutter Aviation already operates at Phoenix Sky Harbor, Albuquerque, El Paso, and San Antonio
  • The Colorado Springs addition fills a geographic gap in the Rocky Mountain corridor, targeting the growing number of corporate aircraft operators based in the Front Range region
  • Why it matters: Business aviation MRO is experiencing a capacity squeeze parallel to commercial aviation — record bizjet utilisation rates mean maintenance events are taking longer and shop queues are extending
