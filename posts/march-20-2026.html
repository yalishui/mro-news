Executive Snapshot

The week of March 17–20 delivered a dense slate of MRO activity: a landmark China engine deal by Lufthansa Technik, a major U.S. facility inauguration by Safran, India's state MRO arm announcing two new greenfield hangars, and the Iran conflict entering the industry risk calculus for the first time. Structurally, the Oliver Wyman 2026–2036 forecast raised the 10-year MRO spend figure to a near-$200B horizon, while a Cirium webinar showed Asia-Pacific consolidating its dominance in heavy maintenance. Supply chain stress remains the sector's defining theme, with IATA and IATP now formalizing a joint mitigation strategy backed by quantified cost data.

Oliver Wyman 2026–2036 Fleet & MRO Market Forecast Oliver Wyman

  • Global MRO spend reached $136 billion in 2025 — up 8% from $126B in 2024 — and is forecast to approach $193 billion by the end of the 2030s, nearly double the 2019 pre-pandemic baseline.
  • The global active commercial fleet (ex-Russia) stands at ~30,000 aircraft, projected to reach ~41,000 by 2036 at a CAGR of 3.2%. This growth pace is ~6 years behind pre-COVID trajectories.
  • Outstanding orders total ~17,000 aircraft, representing more than 12 years of backlog at current production rates.
  • Fleet average age has risen to nearly 13 years (up ~1.5 years since 2024), directly intensifying MRO demand.
  • India leads all markets in fleet growth rate (CAGR ~7.1%); China leads in absolute fleet additions; Middle East at ~5% CAGR.
  • 2025 passenger demand hit 5.2 billion passengers with RPKs 9% above the 2019 pre-COVID peak — yet aircraft supply constraints prevent capacity from keeping pace.

Cirium Webinar: MRO Market Shakeup (March 18) Cirium

  • Asia-Pacific now handles 50–70% of global heavy maintenance work and holds a 56% share of the widebody C-check market.
  • Global MRO line capacity grew 5% from 2019–2025; Europe +28%, North America −20%.
  • Market concentration is tightening: just 35 MRO providers are expected to handle ~50% of all heavy events in 2025.
  • Narrow-body maintenance is more balanced: North America 34%, Asia 34%, Europe 28%.
  • Latin America identified as an emerging narrowbody MRO market.

AFI KLM E&M LEAP Strategy Aviation Week

  • AFI KLM E&M is accelerating LEAP-1A and LEAP-1B MRO capacity with upgraded test cell facilities in Paris (CDG) and Amsterdam (AMS) now fully operational for comprehensive LEAP performance testing.
  • Company received its first LEAP-1B spare engine in Amsterdam in February 2026, part of a commitment to purchase up to 10 LEAP spare engines to eliminate AOG disruption for premier customers.
  • AFI KLM E&M–AerCap LEAP engine leasing JV negotiations remain in progress.

Regional Expansion Activities

Lufthansa Technik × Juneyao Group — Landmark China CFM56 Deal LHT Press Release MRO Business Today

  • LHT signed an exclusive, long-term comprehensive engine overhaul agreement with China's Juneyao Group covering 40+ engine events across Juneyao Air (legacy carrier) and 9 Air (LCC subsidiary).
  • Engine types: CFM56-5B and CFM56-7B. Services include full overhaul, engine condition monitoring, and engineering consultancy, executed at LHT's Hamburg facility.
  • Described as LHT's largest-ever engine services commitment from a Chinese customer and the first full engine overhaul partnership despite a 10-year component/line-services history.
  • LHT Asia-Pacific SVP Dennis Kohr called it "a huge honour" — reflects rapid growth of Chinese aviation and its rising technical requirements.

HAECO × ANA Line Maintenance Extension to 2030 HAECO Press Release

  • HAECO extended its line maintenance partnership with All Nippon Airways (ANA) in Hong Kong through 2030, covering technical and non-technical services for ANA's Boeing 787, 767, and 777F fleet.
  • Partnership spans more than two decades — a flagship long-haul widebody line-maintenance contract at Hong Kong International Airport.

Air Mauritius × AFI KLM E&M A350 Agreement Aviation Week

  • Air Mauritius signed a multi-year extension with AFI KLM E&M for A350 component and APU pool support.
  • Reflects growing African and Indian Ocean airline reliance on European MRO majors for next-generation widebody support.

AIESL — India Greenfield MRO at Noida & Navi Mumbai ePlane AI

  • AI Engineering Services Ltd (AIESL) announced plans to invest ₹200–300 crore (~$24–36M USD) in new MRO facilities at two under-construction airports:
    • Noida International Airport (Jewar): One widebody hangar (~₹100–150 crore)
    • Navi Mumbai International Airport: One widebody hangar (~₹100–150 crore)
  • Estimated operational within two years, subject to land acquisition and regulatory approvals.
  • Context: India's MRO market projected to grow from $1.7B (2021) to over $4B by 2031, yet ~90% of heavy maintenance is still outsourced to Singapore, Dubai, and Colombo.
  • AIESL currently maintains ~20 aircraft/day across 6 bases, including 5–6 widebodies.

GA Telesis Engine Services — Mongolia CFM56 Certification GA Telesis

  • Finland-based GATES received certification from Mongolia's Civil Aviation Authority, establishing a new MRO partnership with MIAT Mongolian Airlines for CFM56-7B engine overhaul support.
  • Adds to GATES' growing regulatory portfolio: FAA, EASA, CAAC, TCCA, GACA.

Safran Electrical & Power — Sarasota, FL Facility Inauguration Safran Press Release Breitflyte

  • Safran inaugurated an expanded MRO campus in Sarasota, Florida now totaling 140,000 sq ft, consolidating all electrical systems MRO from the former Thales Orlando site (acquired October 2023).
  • Services: generators, power distribution/protection, aircraft emergency systems, rotating machinery, and lithium battery systems.
  • Facility originally established 1978; acquired by Safran 2015. Safran supports ~11,000 U.S. jobs across 25 states.

Supply Chain Developments

IATA × IATP Joint Supply Chain Strategy Aviation Week IATA

  • In a landmark first, IATA and the International Airlines Technical Pool (IATP) formalized a joint strategy following the IATP Malta conference (March 7–11).
  • 2025 supply chain disruptions cost the airline industry $11.3 billion:
    • $4.2B — excess fuel burn from delayed fleet retirements
    • $3.1B — extra maintenance costs
    • $2.6B — additional lease costs
    • $1.4B — excess service costs
  • Four-pillar mitigation strategy:
    • Open up aftermarket options (reduce OEM exclusivity barriers)
    • Expand use of PMA and alternative certified parts
    • Improve parts demand forecasting (tackle manufacturer-side opacity)
    • Strengthen anti-counterfeit part certification programs
  • IATA Board has approved phased (short/medium/long-term) action documents. Next steps involve regulatory engagement and OEM collaboration.

Magnetic Group CFM Engine Expansion vs. Bottlenecks Aviation Week

  • Estonia-based Magnetic Engines is scaling up heavier CFM56 workscopes (including core performance restoration) in 2026, targeting full in-house CFM56 overhaul capability following arrival of in-house balancing machine in Q2 2026.
  • Plans to launch LEAP-1A/1B quick-turn shop visits from Tallinn by 2027.
  • Key bottlenecks flagged:
    • European test cell scarcity: engines sent to third-party facilities face 4–6 month queue
    • Hot-section parts shortage: LEAP shop visits average 7–10 months due to thin USM supply
    • MRO shops shifting capacity from CFM56 to LEAP is reducing CFM56 availability, echoing the 2017–2019 squeeze

SkySelect AI Procurement — $9M Raise SkySelect

  • Estonian-founded SkySelect raised $9 million (co-led by Verb Ventures and RockCreek) to expand its AI-powered aircraft parts sourcing platform.
  • Platform uses specialized AI to help airlines and MRO providers reduce AOG delays and excess inventory by optimizing sourcing across fragmented global supply chains.

Major Challenges and Solutions

Iran Conflict — Limited but Monitored MRO Impact Aviation Week / RBC

RBC Capital Markets analysts (Aviation Week, March 19, Sean Broderick) assess the ongoing Iran conflict does not yet represent a major MRO demand threat. Near-term risks: certain spare part sales and short-cycle aftermarket activity. Middle Eastern carriers may defer maintenance or adjust inventory. Industry fundamentals are protective: load factor at 84%, fleet avg age 14.6 years (RBC), severely constrained new aircraft supply. Escalation threshold: sustained conflict over multiple months + persistent high fuel prices would be needed to trigger significant demand reduction. Some carriers (e.g., British Airways) are adding long-haul capacity to capture displaced passengers — potentially adding to MRO demand.

Engine Turnaround Time / European Test Cell Scarcity

Engine TAT continues to be the #1 operational risk. European MRO shops face 4–6 month waits for third-party test cell access. The capacity migration from CFM56 to LEAP is creating secondary supply gaps for CFM56 operators. Solutions: in-house test infrastructure investment (Magnetic Group, Q2 2026 balancing machine), modular swap strategies, strategic USM/LLP pre-positioning.

LEAP Engine Long Shop Visit Cycles

LEAP-1A/1B shop visit turnaround currently averages 7–10 months due to parts scarcity. USM availability is thin because the engines are still relatively young in the fleet and few have been retired. This will be the dominant MRO headache of the late 2020s as LEAP shop visits peak. Magnetic Group and AFI KLM E&M are among the first independents investing ahead of demand.

India MRO Self-Sufficiency Gap

Despite a booming domestic aviation market (1,000+ aircraft on order), India still sends ~90% of heavy maintenance work overseas. AIESL's new Noida/Navi Mumbai proposals and Akasa Air's domestic MRO facility (announced March 14) are part of a larger push to develop indigenous capability. Financial viability concerns remain as AIESL's maintenance revenue has shown slight decline against rising competition from airline-internal MRO divisions.
