Market Updates and Growth Trends

Setna iO Expands Credit Facility to $550 Million — Signals Aggressive Aftermarket Growth

  • Setna iO — the aviation aftermarket and MRO platform — has successfully amended and expanded its commercial credit facility to $550 million in total borrowing capacity (Setna, April 7; AviTrader, April 7)
  • The facility is supported by six top-tier commercial banks, reflecting high institutional confidence in Setna iO's operating model and long-term strategy
  • CEO David Chaimovitz: "This milestone reflects the strength of our business today... We know we are still in the early innings of what Setna can become, and our ambition is clear: we will continue building Setna into a global leader in our space."
  • Subsidiaries set to benefit: Setnix, Setnix UK, PartsLab, Zulu Global, Landing Gear Technologies (LGT), and J&C Aero (soon to join the portfolio)
  • Strategic context: Setna iO's rapid build-out — combining parts trading, MRO services, landing gear, and now J&C Aero's capabilities — mirrors the consolidation theme dominating the aftermarket in 2026
  • Zulu Global note: Florida expansion is underway; new South Florida facility will be approximately 3× the size of current operations
  • Why it matters: A $550M credit line is a significant financial commitment that positions Setna iO to move quickly on aircraft purchases, teardown programmes, and MRO capacity investments — at exactly the moment the market is most capacity-constrained

Marubeni Acquires 100% of DASI — Japanese Industrial Giant Enters US Aviation Parts Market

  • Marubeni Corporation (Japan) has acquired 100% ownership of DASI, the Miami-based aviation inventory specialist (Aviation Week, April 7)
  • DASI (formerly affiliated with Mesa Airlines' CRJ fleet transition) is a parts-trading and inventory management platform with established relationships in the regional jet aftermarket
  • Marubeni's investment thesis: Deep digital infrastructure investment + expansion of new spare parts availability + acceleration of DASI's surplus inventory channel
  • Context: DASI's earlier April 1 deal with Mesa Airlines — acquiring inventory covering Mesa's entire CRJ700 and CRJ900 fleet — set up a strong asset base that Marubeni is now acquiring control of
  • Why it matters: Marubeni's entry represents a new class of strategic acquirer in the US aviation aftermarket — large Japanese industrials who can provide patient, balance-sheet-backed capital to parts platforms. The trend of non-traditional aerospace investors entering MRO/parts is accelerating

Regional Expansion Activities

HEICO Acquires 80% of Sherwood Avionics — Deepens Defense MRO Footprint

  • HEICO Corporation (NYSE: HEI) announced that its Flight Support Group has acquired an 80% majority stake in Sherwood Avionics and Accessories, Inc., with the remaining 20% retained by Sherwood's management team (HEICO press release, April 7; MRO Business Today, April 8)
  • Financial terms: Not disclosed; HEICO expects the acquisition to be accretive to earnings within the first year
  • Sherwood's capabilities: FAA and EASA Part 145 certified repair station, near Opa-locka Airport, Florida; ~150 employees; ~70,000 sq. ft. across two facilities
  • Core MRO services: APUs, landing gear systems (wheels & brakes), hydraulic/pneumatic systems, fuel systems, lighting, avionics — plus OEM-authorised distribution and engineering support
  • Key defense platforms: C-130 Hercules, F-15 Eagle, F-16 Fighting Falcon, CH-47 Chinook, UH-60 Black Hawk
  • Strategic rationale: HEICO gains entrenched OEM relationships and government customer access in the defense MRO space — a segment growing as military fleet sizes expand globally
  • Why it matters: HEICO's disciplined tuck-in acquisition model continues to build out its FSG's Part 145 defense repair network. Back-to-back defense acquisitions signal that HEICO sees defense MRO as an underserved growth vector alongside its dominant commercial PMA parts business

Supply Chain Developments

AFRA and Aethos Launch AMR Award 2026 — Sustainable Recycling Innovation in Focus

  • The Aircraft Fleet Recycling Association (AFRA) and Aethos have launched the 2026 edition of the Aircraft Material Recycling (AMR) Award — calling for projects that advance sustainable aviation recycling (Aethos, April 7)
  • First launched in 2025, the AMR Award recognises innovative solutions for materials left behind after aircraft disassembly — a growing challenge as teardown volumes accelerate in 2026
  • Deadline: Applications accepted until May 1, 2026; winners will be announced at the Aethos Catalyst event
  • Why it matters: With aircraft teardown rates at multi-year highs (driven by new-build delays and younger aircraft entering the USM market), the sustainable management of post-disassembly materials is gaining regulatory and commercial attention

USM Demand Surge — Teardown Programme Expansion Continues Across the Industry

  • Multiple operators and trading firms are expanding teardown programmes in April, responding to:
