MRO Americas 2026 Closes After Record-Burning Three Days

The 30th anniversary edition of MRO Americas concluded at Orlando's Orange County Convention Center, drawing over 15,000 attendees and 700+ exhibitors across three days (April 21–23). The event featured more contract signings and announcements than any previous edition, with the GTF engine MRO bottleneck, AI-driven procurement, workforce development, and industry consolidation dominating discussions.

🌏

GE Aerospace Announces Lifting Futures Workforce Expansion

GE Aerospace, through the GE Aerospace Foundation, announced a new round of investments under its Lifting Futures program — a $30 million, five-year commitment to add 10,000 skilled workers to advanced manufacturing and MRO by 2030. The new round targets five metropolitan areas:

LocationInvestmentFocus

GE cited an industry shortage of approximately 5,000 certificated aviation maintenance technicians as the driving force behind the investment. Specific partner organizations within the five locations will be announced by fall 2026.

Airbus Secures Digital Product Deals with Delta and JetBlue

Airbus announced two significant digital agreements at the show:

  • Delta Air Lines — Descent Profile Optimization (DPO): Deployed across Delta's 270-aircraft Airbus fleet — the largest DPO retrofit on a mixed Airbus fleet to date. DPO optimizes vertical flight profiles to reduce approach fuel consumption.
  • JetBlue — Enhanced Electronic Instrument System + Skywise: Cockpit display upgrade on 46 A320s, plus adoption of Airbus subsidiary Skywise's Fleet Performance+ solution.

💡

Lufthansa Technik Signs Aviatar and Component Contracts

  • Air Transat: Introduced Aviatar's Electronic Technical Logbook, including the new Digital Cabin Logbook application, across its A321 and A330 fleet. Air Transat is the first customer to operate the digital system in both the cockpit and cabin.
  • Woodward: Elite Licensed Repair Service Facility Agreement for CFM Leap-1A and -1B components (fuel controls, valves, actuators).

HEICO Presents at MRO Americas — No New Deals Announced

HEICO Corporation (NYSE: HEI) VP of HPG Technical Services Patrick Markham presented at MRO Americas, discussing the company's MRO capabilities and aftermarket positioning. No new deals or product announcements were made. HEICO's FY2026 Q1 results showed sales of $1.179 billion and net income of $190 million. Analysts project revenue reaching $6 billion by 2029, with key risks including OEM pressure on PMA parts and the threat of additive manufacturing.

FCAH Aerospace Rebrands as "First Class Air," Signs Boeing Deal

  • Rebranding: FCAH Aerospace rebranded as "First Class Air" and launched a unified integration platform consolidating its operating companies: Cargo Repair, First Class Air Support, Cobalt Aero Services, Innodyne Systems, and Survival Products.
  • Boeing Distribution: Survival Products signed a 10-year exclusive distribution agreement adding its life raft and vest products to Boeing's portfolio.

Dedienne Renews Collins Nacelle Tooling License

Dedienne Aerospace renewed its exclusive license with Collins Aerospace for legacy and new-generation nacelle tooling, including ground support equipment and related services.

📋
⚠️

DTX Aerospace and Liebherr Sign ERJ Landing Gear Agreement

DTX Aerospace signed a license service agreement with Liebherr-Aerospace to perform maintenance on Embraer ERJ family nose landing gear components from its facility in Rio de Janeiro — expanding Latin American MRO capability for regional jet landing gear.

📋
⚠️

Fokker Services Group Integrates SkyLog-25 Recorder

Fokker Services Group signed a commercial agreement with KGB Aviation Solutions to integrate the SkyLog-25 flight data and cockpit voice recorder across multiple aircraft platforms. The solution addresses obsolescence challenges and high maintenance costs for aging legacy fleet recorders.

📋
⚠️

ILS Launches New Digital Capabilities

Inventory Locator Service (ILS) introduced two new digital tools:

  • Block Aero Technologies Integration: API connectivity with Block Aero's blockchain-based aviation asset management software to reduce fragmented processes and improve data flow.
  • Digital Catalog: New capability allowing suppliers to extend inventory visibility in a branded, searchable format with a direct path for buyers to engage.
📋
⚠️

AkzoNobel and Donecle Introduce Drone Coatings Inspection Tech

AkzoNobel and Donecle introduced the Iris Coatings Management eXpert tool, which uses a sensor mounted on a Donecle drone to capture data on dry film thickness, color data, and gloss measurements to evaluate coating performance — combining automated drone inspection with coatings intelligence.

📋
⚠️

Jazz Aviation Signs Embraer Inventory Support Contract

Embraer signed a spare parts inventory support agreement with Jazz Aviation, which operates 25 E175s. Jazz becomes the first Canadian customer of Embraer's Collaborative Inventory Planning program.
