  • Narrowbody production: 103 units (77.4% of total; down 4 units from March)
  • Widebody production: 24 units (+50% MoM from 16; driven by Airbus A350 and Boeing 787 acceleration)
  • YTD production through April 2026: 462 units (narrowbody 371 / 80.3%; widebody 75 / 16.2%)

  • Airbus April deliveries: ~60 units (56.1% of combined total)
  • Boeing April deliveries: ~47 units (implied)

  • All segments combined average lead time: 72 days

  • Q3 2026 price moderation scenario: $0.80–1.20/gallon drop possible if supply improves

Supply Chain Developments

Airbus Supply Chain Pressures Persist

  • Q1 2026 revenue decline: 11% (ePlane AI analysis of Airbus results)
  • Contributing factors: Insufficient Pratt & Whitney GTF engine deliveries, A320neo panel quality issues, supply chain disruptions, slowed delivery pace
  • A320neo production-to-delivery lead time increase: 31 days in April (up from 24 in March) — reflecting undelivered inventory buildup
  • Full-year 2026 guidance maintained: ~870 deliveries; back-loaded delivery pattern expected

Boeing-Specific Risks: U.S.-China Geopolitical Tensions

  • Key risk: Ongoing U.S.-China geopolitical tensions threatening Asia-Pacific delivery schedules and long-term order books
  • Aircraft sales to China tied to broader bilateral trade negotiations
  • Financial outlook sensitive to shifts in U.S. foreign policy
  • MRO implication: Delayed deliveries extend legacy fleet lifespan, sustaining aftermarket demand but deferring new-aircraft MRO revenue streams

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Jet Fuel — Structural Shift in MRO Demand

  • MRO demand base-case trim: 4–7% in 2026 (line maintenance and legacy-engine shop visits see steepest contraction)
  • MRO demand bifurcation:
  • ↓ Line maintenance (lower utilization), routine engine shop visits, legacy-engine shop visits
  • ↑ Heavy checks pulled forward (fuel-saving mods), engine performance restoration, SAF compatibility testing, digital-twin predictive maintenance
  • Forward-looking: Next 90 days critical — Hormuz de-escalation, demand elasticity, and hedging renaissance will determine if this is temporary re-phasing or permanent recalibration

Challenge 2: Aircraft Production-Delivery Gap

  • April 2026 narrowbody lead time: 31 days (up 29.2% from March) — reflecting A320neo bottlenecks and undelivered inventory
  • Widebody improvement: 23 days (resolving backlog of stored 787s, enhanced production)
  • MRO implication: Delayed deliveries sustain aftermarket demand for legacy fleets; but create future demand volatility risk when deliveries normalize

Challenge 3: Workforce Shortage — No Near-Term Solution

  • 66% of operators rate certified technician hiring as "moderately to very difficult" (Oliver Wyman 2026)
  • >50% expect attrition to worsen in 2026
  • 2025 labor cost inflation: 5.5–6.0% (line/base/engine), vs. ~3.0% pre-COVID baseline
  • GE Aerospace workforce investments: Millions of dollars across U.S., Europe, Asia for MRO and manufacturing workforce development
