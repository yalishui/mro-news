Technology and Investment News

AI Adoption in MRO — Experimentation Phase but Growing Confidence

  • Oliver Wyman 2026 Survey: 58% of MRO operators still at "experimental" AI stage (unchanged from prior year); however, two-thirds of those experimenting report results meeting or exceeding expectations.
  • Primary barrier: Data quality and availability — prerequisite for meaningful AI deployment.
  • Investment gap identified: 50% plan AI investment over next 5 years; only 33% planning IT infrastructure investment — potential bottleneck as AI initiatives scale.
  • GE Aerospace + Waygate Technologies: AI-guided engine borescope inspection tool debuted at MRO Americas 2026 — aims to standardize inspection consistency.

Workforce Development — Chronic Shortage Drives Industry-Wide Response

  • Labor rate inflation: 5.5–6.0% across most categories (vs. ~3.0% pre-pandemic); stubbornly high for engines and components.
  • Two-thirds of respondents find it moderately to very challenging to find aircraft technicians/mechanics.
  • >50% expect attrition to worsen next year.
  • Solutions deployed: AMT donations (GA Telesis $4.1M Trent 800 donation to Broward College; Duncan Aviation TFE731 donation to Lincoln North Star HS); accelerated training pathways; apprenticeship programs.
  • MRO SouthEast Asia (May 13–14, Kuala Lumpur) will address regional workforce challenges as a key agenda item.

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Next-Gen Engine Complexity Driving Cost Overruns — Systemic Issue

  • Two-thirds of operators report GTF/LEAP shop costs exceeding expectations by 21%+; 25% exceed by 50%+.
  • TATs of 180–200+ days now the norm for narrowbody engines — no relief expected for 3+ years per majority of operators.
  • Solutions: Fleet planning adjustments; predictive maintenance to pre-empt failures; OEM-MRO collaboration on shop process optimization; AI-powered inspection and lifecycle management tools.

Challenge 2: USM Market Maturity — Balancing Supply with Fleet Aging

  • As next-gen platforms (A320neo, 737 MAX) enter teardown phase, MRO operators must navigate dual markets: extended legacy fleet operations alongside younger next-gen disassembly.
  • USM market participants (APOC Aviation, Executive Jet Support, StandardAero) are scaling rapidly — landing gear, engine modules, and nacelle components seeing strongest demand.
  • Solutions: Strategic inventory buildup; diversification of USM sourcing channels; data-driven lifecycle tracking.

Challenge 3: Integration Execution Risk — Mega M&A

  • VSE–PAG ($2.025B) and AAR restructuring represent two of the largest MRO platform changes of 2026.
  • Integration execution risk is significant: cross-selling, repair insourcing, and procurement efficiencies are key synergy levers.
  • VSE targets >20% Adjusted EBITDA margins through disciplined integration; AAR wind-down of Legacy Commercial Programs ($252.4M LTM sales) over 3–4 years.

Challenge 4: Asia-Pacific Capacity Crunch

  • Fleet growth continues to outpace available MRO infrastructure throughout Asia-Pacific.
  • Regional airlines (especially LCCs) and lessors face mounting pressure securing MRO slots.
  • Solutions: Greenfield MRO facility development (Sanad, Dornier, MAB Engineering); expansion of existing hubs (Singapore, Subang); cross-border partnerships; painting + heavy maintenance one-stop packages.
