Market Updates & Growth Trends

  • Global MRO Market Size: The aviation MRO market is currently valued at $98.38 billion in 2026, with projections reaching $155 billion by 2035 at a CAGR of 5.19% (Business Research Insights). Separate estimates peg the current value between $88.7B and $98.4B depending on methodology.
  • AerSale Q4 2025 Earnings — "Hypercompetitive" Feedstock Market (Aviation Week, March 13)
  • CEO Nicolas Finazzo described the used aircraft and engine purchase market as "hypercompetitive," with AerSale winning only 1 in 10 bids for teardown feedstock.
  • The company invested $100 million in feedstock purchases in 2025 but expects to reduce that figure in 2026 due to persistently elevated prices.
  • 2025 MRO maintenance revenue: $123 million (slight decline YoY, offset by growth in airframe structures and landing gear MRO).
  • Strategy shift for 2026: increase MRO facility utilization, expand USM (used serviceable material) sales, grow component MRO revenue, and expand leasing pool assets.
  • Roswell facility pivoting to aircraft storage and retired fleet handling; Millington facility at full capacity since December 2025, with a signed multi-year regional airline agreement.
  • William Blair Aviation Services Industry Update (March 2026): 2025 ended with strong business aviation momentum; engine bottlenecks and MRO capacity constraints persist. Market expansion is ongoing despite macroeconomic pressures.

Regional Expansion Activities

  • SIAEC Wins 30% Stake in Fujian MRO Venture (Aviation Week, ~2 days ago)
  • Singapore's SIA Engineering Company (SIAEC) successfully acquired a 30% stake in Arport Aircraft Maintenance & Engineering (Fujian) Co., Ltd. (Arport AME) — a unit of Xiamen Import Group — for US$17.9 million (RMB 129 million / SGD 23.86 million).
  • The deal expands SIAEC's aircraft maintenance footprint into China's Fujian province, serving over 80 global carriers.
  • This represents a calculated entry into the fast-growing Chinese domestic MRO market as regional air travel demand surges.
  • EirTrade Aviation to Quintuple Knock Teardown Facility (Aviation Week, March 12)
  • Ireland-based EirTrade received full planning permission from Mayo County Council to expand its facility at Ireland West Airport Knock.
  • Current facility: 2,100 sqm → Planned expansion: 10,500 sqm (5× increase).
  • New facility will include: a narrow-body aircraft hangar (teardown + line maintenance), engine storage, workshop, and packaging areas.
  • Construction targeted for late 2026 start.
  • EirTrade recently acquired two low-cycle Airbus A320neos (ex-Spirit Airlines) for disassembly, reflecting strong demand for used parts.
  • Senior VP Steven Trowell: "The expansion of our facility to increase performance capacity is a testament to our commitment to deliver high-quality support to operators, owners, and lessors worldwide."
  • Boeing & SCAT Airlines Begin Construction of MRO Center in Kazakhstan (Aviation Week, March 9)
  • Boeing and SCAT Airlines have broken ground on a major MRO center in Shymkent, Kazakhstan, servicing 737, 757, and 767 aircraft.
  • Expected to become the largest MRO facility in Central Asia.
  • Kalitta Air Partners with Sharp Technics K (South Korea) (Aviation Week, March 11)
  • Kalitta Air has established a maintenance partnership with Korean MRO firm Sharp Technics K for heavy maintenance checks on its Boeing 777 fleet, beginning May 2026.
  • Airhub Aviation & AerCap Sign Maintenance Agreement (Aviation Week, March 10)
  • Lithuania-based Airhub Aviation will provide base maintenance and aircraft redelivery services for lessor AerCap, expanding regional MRO capacity in the Baltic.
  • HAECO Signs Line Maintenance Contract with Air India (Aviation Week, March 13)
  • HAECO will provide line maintenance services for Air India's widebody fleet (A350, B777, B787) at Shanghai Pudong International Airport, contract valid through 2029.
  • Middle East MRO Growth Drives Talent Initiatives (Aviation Week, featured article)
  • The booming Middle East MRO sector is driving concerted regional efforts to recruit and upskill aviation maintenance talent, flagged as both a growth opportunity and a systemic challenge.

Supply Chain Developments

  • Asia-Pacific Supply Chain Localization Accelerates (Aviation Week, March 10)
  • At AeroEngines Asia-Pacific 2026 in Hong Kong, panelists identified lengthy engine turnaround times (TAT) as the top risk — significantly longer than pre-pandemic norms.
  • Key supply chain strategies being deployed:
  • Barnes Aerospace expanding regional presence in Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia to shorten TAT and support P&W, CFM, GE, and Rolls-Royce programs.
  • Magnetic Group establishing regional hubs in Miami, New Delhi, and Kuala Lumpur; 40–50% of business volume now comes from outside Europe.
  • Modular replacement strategies are replacing deep overhaul approaches — airlines swap entire engine modules to get aircraft back in service faster, requiring tight coordination with downstream module suppliers.
  • USM and PMA parts acceptance rising: Chinese airlines — historically resistant to used serviceable materials and PMA parts — are increasingly adopting them as cost-control measures. Industry commentators note this is "just the beginning" of China's PMA market development.
  • Oliver Wyman analysis (for IATA): Airlines paid ~$3.1 billion in "additional maintenance costs" linked to supply chain constraints in 2025.
  • AerSale Supply Chain Observations (March 13)
  • Feedstock availability (used aircraft/engines for parts harvesting) remains severely constrained across all major operators.
  • Price inflation for teardown candidates is outpacing return on investment for many providers.
  • DASI Acquires Mesa Airlines CRJ Spare Parts Inventory (Aviation Week, March 4)
  • DASI acquired Mesa Airlines' entire CRJ-700/900 spare parts inventory as Mesa transitions its fleet to Embraer E-Jets, injecting supply into the tight CRJ parts market.
  • Corax Doubles Copenhagen Warehouse Capacity (Aviation Week, March 4)
  • Danish component specialist Corax completed a warehouse expansion in Copenhagen, doubling storage capacity to meet parts demand.
  • Ametek MRO Expands Investment as Component Capacity Tightens (Aviation Week, March 3)
  • As post-pandemic aircraft component repair demand accelerates faster than expected, Ametek MRO (Ramsgate, UK) is responding with major investments:
  • £1 million+ invested over two years in chromium emission reduction equipment to meet EU REACH regulations.
  • New evaporator installed to enable water recycling and reduce hazardous waste.
  • Expanded capabilities in landing gear (737-400/-500/-800/-900, E190), hydraulics, and heat exchangers (now including welding and full repairs).
  • Deploying AI-assisted digital tools: video-based repair guides, augmented reality (AR) technician support, and digital traceability systems.
  • Cautiously evaluating 737 MAX component MRO entry; high asset acquisition costs remain a barrier.

Technology & Investment News

  • Pratt & Whitney Speeds GTF AOG Recovery via Robotics (Aviation Week, February 3)
  • Pratt & Whitney has deployed camera-equipped robots at Eagle Services Asia (Singapore) for intake inspection and documentation of GTF engines prior to disassembly — reducing human inspection time and improving consistency.
  • Robotic innovations are being integrated broadly across the GTF MRO network to address the ongoing high-volume shop visit cycle stemming from the PW1000G recall.
  • P&W targets end of decade for full GTF recovery (FlightGlobal, February 2026).
  • AI-Powered Optical Technology Targets Aerospace Inventory Accuracy (Aviation Week, January 12)
  • Inventory management specialist RGIS reports that a major carrier achieved 98.9% inventory accuracy using AI-powered optical scanning technology — a significant improvement over manual counts.
  • MRO organizations are increasingly automating parts visibility to reduce stockouts, improve planning, and cut excess inventory costs.
  • Azul Airlines to Revisit AI for MRO Planning Post-Chapter 11 (Aviation Week, featured)
  • Following its Chapter 11 restructuring, Brazilian carrier Azul is revisiting AI-powered MRO planning tools as part of its operational recovery and cost-efficiency drive.
  • Trax eMRO Selected by Luxury Startup Magnifica Air (Aviation Week, March 11)
  • Pre-launch luxury airline Magnifica Air (planned debut 2027) has selected Trax eMRO and eMobility software for a fully paperless maintenance operation — signaling broader adoption of digital MRO platforms by new entrants.
  • British Airways Acquires Additional 3D Dent Mapping Tools (Aviation Week, March 3)
  • British Airways purchased three additional 8tree dentCHECK units for 3D dent mapping on narrow-body aircraft at Glasgow and London Heathrow — improving damage assessment speed and accuracy.
  • Additive Manufacturing for GTF MRO:
  • Pratt & Whitney has also developed a validated additive manufacturing (AM) repair process for critical GTF components, reducing reliance on traditional casting and machining for certain parts.
  • MRO Industry Leaders Predict 2026 Key Technology Trends (Aviation Week, January 23)
  • Consensus from industry leaders: a stronger push for automation, AI, and data analytics will define 2026 MRO strategy.
  • Focus areas: predictive maintenance, digital twins, enhanced safety outcomes, and quality optimization.
  • 3D printing/additive manufacturing in supply chains, agentic AI for industrial workflows, and MRO expansion into spacecraft support cited as emerging areas.

Major Challenges & Solutions

  • Workforce Shortage — A Persistent Structural Challenge
  • CAAS (Civil Aviation Authority of Singapore) is fast-tracking MRO workforce training under new labor initiatives to close the skills gap.
  • Thrust Flight opened a new FAA-approved aircraft mechanic school (Thrust Institute of Maintenance) at Fort Worth Spinks Airport (March 10) — one of several new training institutions addressing industry-wide shortages.
  • American Airlines donated an APU (formerly on MD-80s) to the Chicago School of Aviation Maintenance to support technician training (March 9).
  • Ametek MRO notes acute difficulty recruiting skilled technicians in coastal/non-industrial UK locations; deploying AR and AI training tools to accelerate onboarding.
  • Engine Turnaround Times Remain the #1 Risk in Asia-Pacific
  • Industry consensus at AeroEngines Asia-Pacific: TAT predictability — not just length — is the primary pain point. Airlines cannot reliably plan fleet availability.
  • Solutions being deployed: regional supply chain hubs, modular swap strategies, increased USM/PMA acceptance.
  • Feedstock Price Inflation Squeezing Part-Out Economics
  • AerSale's experience (1-in-10 bid win rate) illustrates the competitive intensity for used aircraft/engine feedstock.
  • Some buyers are paying prices that industry veterans consider commercially unsustainable long-term, risking a market correction.
  • Environmental Compliance Adding Cost Pressure
  • EU REACH regulations are forcing MRO shops to transition away from chromium and cadmium plating processes, requiring significant capital investment.
  • Ametek MRO's £1M+ environmental upgrade is emblematic of compliance costs facing European MROs.
  • GTF Engine Recall — Long Recovery Timeline
  • Pratt & Whitney's GTF (PW1000G) inspection and shop visit cycle is expected to extend through end of decade, keeping global narrow-body MRO capacity under strain.
  • Robotics, additive manufacturing, and expanded MRO network agreements are key mitigation tools.
