Fleet & Component Deals

DASI Acquires Mesa Airlines CRJ Fleet Inventory to Support Republic Airways Merger

  • DASI (Miami-based aviation inventory and logistics solutions provider) signed an agreement with Mesa Airlines to support its fleet transition and integration into Republic Airways (Aviation Week, April 1)
  • As part of the deal, DASI acquired the entire spare parts inventory covering Mesa's full CRJ700 and CRJ900 fleet
  • Mesa Airlines is in the process of winding down independent operations and merging its regional flying operations under Republic Airways
  • The inventory acquisition covers rotable components, expendable parts, and potentially tooling associated with the CRJ program
  • DASI's role: Will manage redistribution of the Mesa inventory into DASI's customer network, making parts available to other CRJ operators globally
  • Why it matters: As regional carriers consolidate, significant pools of serviceable parts become available — DASI's move positions it as a key redistributor of CRJ assets in a market where the CRJ is still widely operated by 30+ airlines worldwide

AerFin Acquires Fourth Japan Airlines 777-300ER for Teardown

  • AerFin (UK-based aircraft asset specialist) acquired its fourth Boeing 777-300ER previously operated by Japan Airlines, adding to its growing 777 teardown and parts trading programme (Aviation Week, April 1)
  • The aircraft joins three other ex-JAL 777-300ERs that AerFin has processed, significantly deepening its inventory of GE90-115B parts, APU components, and airframe consumables
  • AerFin strategy: The company has identified ex-JAL and ex-Emirates 777-300ERs as a reliable source of high-quality, well-documented components; JAL's stringent maintenance standards mean teardown yields premium serviceable parts
  • Why it matters: GE90-115B shop visit cycle times remain extended due to capacity constraints; operators are increasingly turning to USM (used serviceable material) from well-maintained teardowns to bridge the gap — AerFin's fourth JAL teardown adds meaningful supply to a tight market

Facility & Network Expansions

Solinair Expands to Klagenfurt Airport — Grows MRO Footprint in Central Europe

  • Solinair (Slovenian MRO provider, part of Adria Airways Engineering heritage) has expanded its operations to Klagenfurt Airport (Klagenfurt, Austria), growing its Central European MRO presence (MRO Business Today, April 1)
  • The new Klagenfurt base is Solinair's second Austrian location and adds line and base maintenance capacity for narrowbody aircraft
  • Solinair currently holds EASA Part 145 approval and services primarily A320-family and CRJ aircraft for European regional carriers
  • The Klagenfurt expansion is driven by growing demand from carriers operating in the Central European leisure and charter market
  • Why it matters: Central Europe remains underserved for quality, competitively priced base maintenance — Solinair's expansion is a direct response to demand from Austrian, Slovenian, and Balkan-based operators

StandardAero Expands Agreement with Robinson Helicopter Company StandardAero

  • StandardAero, one of the world's largest independent MRO providers, has expanded its authorised service agreement with Robinson Helicopter Company (Robinson Helicopter / StandardAero, April 2026)
  • The expanded agreement broadens StandardAero's approved scope for Robinson R44 and R66 helicopter maintenance, allowing more locations within StandardAero's network to service Robinson products
  • Why it matters: Helicopter MRO is a growing segment as offshore, EMS, and SAR operators seek consolidated maintenance solutions from providers with both fixed-wing and rotary capabilities

Analyst Note

The Mesa/DASI deal and the AerFin/JAL teardown both point to the same underlying dynamic: accelerating fleet consolidation and retirements are freeing up significant used parts inventory precisely when new-build delays and shop capacity constraints are pushing operators toward USM solutions. Expect this trend to continue through 2026–2027 as ageing A330, 777-200ER, and CRJ fleets accelerate toward teardown.
