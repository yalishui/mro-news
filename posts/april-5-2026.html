Personnel & Leadership

AFI KLM E&M Appoints New KLM Engine Services VP AFI KLM E&M

  • Air France Industries KLM Engineering & Maintenance (AFI KLM E&M) appointed Benoît Rollier as Vice President of KLM Engine Services, effective this month (Aviation Week, April 3)
  • Rollier replaces Martijn de Vries, who transitions to the role of Senior Vice President Commercial at AFI KLM E&M
  • KLM Engine Services is one of Europe's largest engine MRO operations, with capabilities covering CFM56, GE90, V2500, Trent 700, and CF34 families
  • Why it matters: Leadership transitions at major MRO providers often signal strategic pivots — de Vries' move to a commercial SVP role suggests AFI KLM E&M is sharpening its focus on contract growth and customer acquisition ahead of what is expected to be a record year for engine shop visits

Fleet & Component Deals

Innovative Systems Licences Honeywell Generator Technology for B767, KC-46, F-15

  • Innovative Systems (aerospace systems integrator) secured an exclusive licence from Honeywell International covering technical data and tooling for power generators and generator control units on the Boeing 767, KC-46A Pegasus tanker, and F-15 platforms (Aviation Week, April 3)
  • Under the licence, Innovative Systems gains rights to manufacture, repair, and overhaul Honeywell-designed generators for these aircraft — spanning both civil and military variants
  • Platform significance:
  • Boeing 767: Still operated by Delta, United, and multiple cargo operators (UPS, FedEx, Amazon Air); significant MRO demand remaining
  • KC-46A: US Air Force's primary aerial refuelling tanker; a fleet of 68+ aircraft currently in service with more on order — government MRO contract potential
  • F-15: Eagle and Strike Eagle fleets operated by USAF and multiple allied nations; long service life ahead
  • Why it matters: The Honeywell licence gives Innovative Systems a foothold in a niche but strategically important components segment, reducing OEM sole-source dependency for military and cargo operators

Market Intelligence

Indonesia Launches $800M Aviation Leasing Platform — Fleet Growth to Drive MRO Demand

  • Indonesia has launched a national aviation leasing platform with initial capitalisation of $800 million, designed to allow domestic airlines to acquire aircraft through Indonesian entities rather than foreign lessors (MRO Business Today, April 3)
  • The platform is backed by Indonesian state-owned entities and sovereign wealth fund structures
  • MRO implications: A larger domestically-managed Indonesian fleet implies growing demand for local or regional MRO services — Indonesia's Garuda Maintenance Facility AeroAsia (GMF) is positioned as the primary beneficiary
  • Indonesia's commercial aviation fleet is projected to grow significantly through 2030, with Lion Air group, Garuda Indonesia, and Citilink all expanding
  • Why it matters: Southeast Asia's MRO market is one of the fastest-growing globally — Indonesia's leasing platform accelerates fleet growth, which directly translates to more MRO work-packages entering the regional market over the next 3–5 years

Facility & Network Expansions

IAC International Expands into Malta — New European Interior Services Base

  • IAC International (International Aero Coatings, specialising in aircraft painting, interior refurbishment, and cabin conversion) is expanding into Malta, adding the island's Malta International Airport to its European service network (MRO Business Today, April 3)
  • IAC's Malta facility will offer interior refurbishment, cabin upgrades, and aircraft painting for narrowbody aircraft
  • Malta's strategic central-Mediterranean location makes it attractive for European, Middle Eastern, and North African airlines needing interior work done between rotations
  • IAC already has European bases in Shannon (Ireland) and Lake Charles (US); Malta adds a southern European hub
  • Why it matters: Interior and cabin MRO is a growing segment as airlines invest in retrofits (seatback IFE, lie-flat business class upgrades, cabin densification) as part of post-pandemic product refresh programmes
