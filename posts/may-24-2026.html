Market Updates & Growth Trends

  • MTU Maintenance Berlin-Brandenburg achieved a milestone with the first redelivery of a repaired Pratt & Whitney Canada PW800 engine (May 24), following EASA certification in 2025. The Ludwigsfelde, Germany facility is now one of only two facilities worldwide authorized for full PW800 MRO — the other being P&WC's own Bridgeport, West Virginia site. Over 1,000 PW800 engines have been produced, powering 415+ business jets in 30 countries (Gulfstream G500/G600/G400, Dassault Falcon 6X).
  • Liebherr-Aerospace and Finnair signed an MRO agreement for Airbus A350 nose landing gear overhaul at Liebherr's Lindenberg, Germany facility (May 24). Alex Vlielander, Liebherr Chief Customer Officer, noted: "The industry is moving into the next global landing gear overhaul wave." Liebherr serves as both OEM and MRO provider for the A350 nose landing gear.
  • APOC Aviation launched an expanded component exchange pool for airline customers, stocking used serviceable material from recent A320 Family teardowns — including thrust reversers and inlet cowls for V2500 and CFM56-5B engines (May 24). Craig Skilton, VP Components: "Building an exchange pool of narrowbody assets is a cornerstone of APOC's strategy."
  • GE Aerospace began rolling out its 360 Foam Wash jet engine cleaning system across its global MRO network (May 24), expanding on-wing engine care capabilities.

🌏

Regional Expansion Activities

  • Emirates Engineering completed the first-ever two-class to three-class A380 retrofit on aircraft A6-EUX (May 24), adding Premium Economy for the first time on the A380 upper deck. The new cabin features 76 Business, 56 Premium Economy, and 437 Economy seats (569 total). First route: Dubai–Birmingham (EK 39/40). This is the first of 15 two-class A380s to be renovated, part of a wider fleet retrofit of 95 aircraft (42 A380 + 53 B777).
  • MTU Berlin-Brandenburg PW800 capability: The facility's expansion into PW800 MRO strengthens Europe's business jet engine MRO infrastructure, reducing reliance on North American shop visits for European and Middle Eastern Gulfstream and Falcon operators.

🔗

Supply Chain Developments

  • PW800 MRO capacity: With only two authorized facilities globally, the MTU Berlin-Brandenburg addition effectively doubles worldwide PW800 MRO capacity — critical as the G500/G600 fleet matures and shop visits increase.
  • A320 component exchange: APOC's expanded exchange pool addresses the persistent shortage of rotable components for the world's largest narrowbody fleet. Teardown-sourced USM provides a faster, more cost-effective alternative to new OEM parts.
  • A350 landing gear overhaul wave: Liebherr's Finnair contract signals the beginning of the first major landing gear overhaul cycle for the A350 fleet, which entered service in 2015. Airlines will increasingly need to schedule these heavy checks through the late 2020s.
