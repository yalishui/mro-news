MRO Americas 2026 Opens in Orlando — 30th Anniversary Edition

Aviation Week Network's MRO Americas 2026 officially opened at the Orange County Convention Center in Orlando, Florida, marking the event's 30th year. The three-day conference and exhibition (April 21–23) is the world's largest gathering of the aviation maintenance, repair, and overhaul community, co-located with the Military Aviation Logistics & Maintenance Symposium (MALMS). Over 15,000 attendees and 700+ exhibitors are expected.

Pratt & Whitney Invests $100M+ to Expand U.S. GTF MRO Footprint

Pratt & Whitney, an RTX (NYSE: RTX) business, announced investments of more than $100 million across three U.S. MRO sites to accelerate GTF engine maintenance capacity and reduce turnaround times:

SiteInvestmentKey Capability
Irving, Texas$78 millionNew 500,000 sq ft Commercial Serviceable Assets facility; USM stock ↑60%+
West Palm Beach, FL$20 million50,000 sq ft expansion; GTF MRO capacity ↑40%; new assembly/disassembly, machining, testing equipment
Springdale, Arkansas$4.7 million7,000 sq ft expansion; GTF additive manufacturing repairs — process time ↓60%+

Additionally, P&W's earlier 2026 investment in Columbus, Georgia ($70M, 81,000 sq ft) increased annual capacity by 25%+.

Delta TechOps Signs Two Major Third-Party MRO Agreements

Delta TechOps announced two significant deals:

  • LATAM Airlines Brasil: Long-term collaboration initially focused on Airbus A320 component repair — a strategic expansion into the South American market.
  • IndiGo: Eight-year exclusive CFM56-5B maintenance contract — one of the largest narrowbody engine MRO deals announced at the show, reinforcing Delta's position as a leading third-party engine MRO provider.

💡

AFI KLM E&M Announces Four Contracts

AFI KLM E&M and its subsidiaries (Epcor, Barfield) signed multiple agreements:

  • Woodward: Elite Licensed Repair Service Facility for CFM Leap components (fuel controls, actuators, pumps, valves).
  • Air Canada (via Epcor): 15-year extension for APS5000 APU support.
  • AerFin (via Barfield): 3-year component repair covering A320-family, A330, Boeing, and Embraer 170/190.
  • JetBlue (via Barfield): Technical and logistical support for Donecle drone inspection rollout.

AAR Launches Airvoyant AI Subsidiary, Signs Two Major Deals

AAR Corp (NYSE: AIR) made three significant announcements:

1. Airvoyant Launch: New subsidiary focused on automating aviation procurement using agentic artificial intelligence — a first-of-its-kind AI-driven parts sourcing platform.

2. Woodward Distribution: Multi-year commercial distribution agreement as preferred distributor for CFM Leap, GE GEnx, and CF34 engine consumable parts.

3. U.S. Navy/Marine Corps: ~$305 million contract for contractor logistics support on the Boeing C-40A fleet.

Aeromexico Goes Live with Flydocs Digital Records

Aeromexico rolled out Flydocs' Digital Records Management (DRM) across its ~170-aircraft fleet via a five-year partnership. The integration connects Flydocs DRM with Swiss-AS AMOS software for seamless documentation workflows.

Acorn Capital Management Acquires Majority Stake in MTI Aviation

Private equity firm Acorn Capital Management announced acquisition of a majority interest in MTI Aviation, a specialist in component repair, overhaul, and parts distribution (electro-mechanical, hydraulics, pneumatics, power generation). The deal, announced April 20, represents Acorn's first platform investment in Fund VI. Founder/CEO Jorge Mejia remains a significant shareholder and continues leading the company. Transaction expected to close in the coming weeks.

Market & Business Implications

  • GTF engine MRO remains the industry's top capacity bottleneck. P&W's $100M+ investment, combined with the earlier Columbus, GA expansion ($70M), signals sustained urgency to reduce turnaround times for GTF operators.
  • AI enters aviation procurement. AAR's Airvoyant launch is the most notable innovation at the show — agentic AI applied to parts sourcing could be transformative if it proves effective.
  • Consolidation accelerates. Acorn Capital's MTI Aviation acquisition and the ongoing VSE–PAG $2B deal (announced January, closing pending) underscore PE appetite for aviation aftermarket platforms.
  • Delta TechOps expands globally. The LATAM and IndiGo deals position Delta as a truly global third-party MRO provider, not just a North American player.
  • Digital transformation gains traction. Flydocs at Aeromexico, Airbus DPO at Delta, and LHT Aviatar at Air Transat all signal airlines' growing commitment to digital tools.
