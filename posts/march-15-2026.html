Market Updates & Growth Trends

  • Aircraft MRO market on robust trajectory: According to a BriefingWire market brief published March 11, the global Aircraft MRO market continues its steady growth trajectory driven by aging fleets, record airline load factors, and prolonged new-aircraft delivery backlogs that force operators to extend maintenance cycles on in-service aircraft.
  • CFM LEAP demand escalating: Aviation Week's 2026 industry forecast (updated this week) noted that as Pratt & Whitney GTF-related AOG incidents begin to ease, strain is visibly shifting toward CFM LEAP-1A/1B aircraft due to emerging durability issues, supply chain constraints, and rising shop-visit volumes — signaling a fresh wave of demand for LEAP MRO capacity through the end of the decade.
  • Longer shop visits normalizing into planning cycles: GE Aerospace and Safran (CFM International) have raised their short-term annual shop-visit forecasts through 2028, acknowledging that the elevated volume of CFM56 inductions is persisting longer than previously projected due to new-aircraft delivery delays.
  • Deloitte 2026 Aerospace & Defense Outlook (published March 14) reinforced the sector's resilience: aftermarket revenues continue to outpace OEM revenues as operators prioritize life extension over replacement, a structural dynamic favoring MRO providers through at least 2030.

Regional Expansion Activities

India — Major Milestone

  • Akasa Air × Noida International Airport (Jewar) MRO Partnership (March 14)
  • India's fastest-growing carrier, Akasa Air (current fleet: ~35 aircraft, 226 on order), announced a strategic partnership with Noida International Airport (NIA) in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh, to establish Akasa's first domestic MRO facility.
  • Facility scope: line maintenance, scheduled aircraft checks, and technical services.
  • Strategic rationale: India currently sends the majority of deep-maintenance work overseas; the NIA facility directly addresses that dependency while creating high-skilled technical jobs in the NCR region.
  • Aligns with India's government push to build domestic aviation infrastructure ahead of NIA's anticipated opening.

China — Regulatory Certification

  • General MRO Aerospace (GMA) receives CAAC certification (March 13)
  • Miami, FL-based GMA — already FAA and EASA certified — received formal authorization from China's Civil Aviation Administration (CAAC) to perform component MRO for Chinese-registered aircraft.
  • GMA President Jonathan Cornell called it "a testament to our quality systems and technical expertise."
  • Positions GMA to serve Chinese operators, lessors, and MRO providers as the Asia-Pacific market continues its rapid fleet expansion.

Europe — Cargo Conversion Momentum

  • EFW signs new A330-300 P2F conversion contract with APAL (March 12–13)
  • Elbe Flugzeugwerke (EFW) — the Dresden-based Airbus/ST Engineering joint venture — secured a contract with Hong Kong-based Asia Pacific Aviation Leasing Group (APAL) for the conversion of one Airbus A330-300 passenger-to-freighter (P2F) aircraft.
  • EFW's first conversion deal with APAL; reflects continued appetite for freighter conversions amid robust air cargo demand and constrained new freighter deliveries.

Central Asia — Landmark Groundbreaking (Continued Progress)

  • Boeing × SCAT Airlines MRO Center, Shymkent, Kazakhstan: Construction continues following the March 9 groundbreaking. The ~50,000 sqm facility — Central Asia's largest planned MRO center — will service Boeing 737, 757, 767, and 777 variants and is positioned as a regional maintenance hub.

Ireland — Teardown Facility Expansion (Approval Confirmed)

  • EirTrade Aviation has received planning permission to expand its Knock teardown/parts-reclamation facility from 2,100 sqm to 10,500 sqm (a 5x increase), with construction expected to begin this year.

Supply Chain Developments

  • GE Aerospace invests $33M in CFM56 turbine blade manufacturing (March 12, Aviation Week)
  • GE is investing $33 million in its Greenville, South Carolina turbine blade production facility — the only manufacturing site for CFM56 high-pressure turbine (HPT) blades — part of a broader $1 billion U.S. manufacturing investment program.
  • ~$16.5M dedicated to advanced grinding, laser-drilling, and machining technologies for CFM56 PIP-configuration HPT blades, matching techniques already used for LEAP engine blade production.
  • Additional $200 million within the same program targets LEAP aftermarket components, including HPT durability kits and reverse bleed systems.
  • UK supplier ANT Industries (Atherstone, Midlands) welcomed the announcement as a signal of long-term demand security for its supply chain partnerships with GE.
  • Impact: The investment aims to relieve a persistent bottleneck — HPT blade supply is frequently the primary driver of extended CFM56 engine shop-visit turnaround times.
  • IATA reinforces supply chain pressure data (as of this week): Aircraft deliveries stood at approximately 1,254 units in 2024 — roughly 30% below pre-COVID peak levels — keeping the global backlog above 17,000 orders. This delivery shortfall is the primary structural driver of elevated MRO demand, extended TATs, and component scarcity.
  • DASI acquires Mesa Airlines CRJ-700/900 spare parts inventory (March 4): The acquisition provides DASI with a large pool of in-demand regional aircraft components as Mesa transitions its fleet to Embraer E-Jets following its merger with Republic Airways.
  • Corax (Denmark) doubles warehouse capacity (March 4): Third expansion at Copenhagen facility completed, doubling inventory management capacity to better serve European MRO demand for components.

Technology & Investment News

Digital & AI Innovation

  • HAESL (HAECO × Rolls-Royce JV) launches AI-driven MRO tools (March 10, Aviation Week)
  • HAESL — which overhauled ~380 Rolls-Royce engines and ~40 Trent 1000 TEN modules in 2025, representing ~25% of Rolls-Royce's global MRO network volume — is deploying:
  • AI-enhanced borescope inspections for faster, more accurate defect detection
  • Robotic AI surface inspection tools to reduce manual inspection time
  • AI-integrated electronic work cards for real-time workflow tracking
  • HAECO Customer Gateway digital platform expansion to its Xiamen engine shop, providing customers live repair status visibility
  • Customer Gateway has already cut average invoice approval downtime from 6–7 days to 1 day.
  • Planned upgrade to cloud-based SAP S/4HANA by March 2027, creating a unified data foundation for enterprise-wide analytics.
  • Part of HAECO Group's decade-long "Smart Factory" digital roadmap.

Digital MRO Software Adoption

  • Magnifica Air selects Trax eMRO platform (March 11)
  • Florida-based luxury carrier Magnifica Air (set to launch operations in 2027) has chosen Trax's eMRO and eMobility software to run paperless maintenance operations from day one — a notable signal of digital-native MRO becoming baseline expectation for new-entrant carriers.

Conversion & Cargo Investments

Major Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Hypercompetitive Feedstock Market

  • AerSale characterized the used-aircraft and engine feedstock market as "hypercompetitive", winning only 1 in 10 bids for available assets. The company confirmed it is scaling back its planned $100M 2025 feedstock investment in 2026, redirecting capital allocation.
  • Root cause: Fleet retirements have slowed dramatically as airlines extend aircraft lives due to new-build delivery delays, drastically reducing the supply of teardown-ready aircraft and serviceable used parts (USM).

Challenge: Engine Turnaround Times in Asia-Pacific

  • Asia-Pacific carriers continue to experience industry-leading engine TAT delays, identified as the top regional MRO risk. Key drivers: constrained shop capacity, spare parts scarcity, and the volume of GTF-related shop visits.
  • Strategies in response:
  • Modular replacement: airlines swapping serviceable modules instead of waiting for full shop completion
  • USM and PMA adoption accelerating as carriers seek faster access to serviceable parts
  • HAESL's digital toolset investment (see Technology section) directly targets TAT reduction for Trent engines

Challenge: Skilled Workforce Shortages

  • Singapore's CAAS fast-tracks MRO workforce program (March 5): Singapore's Civil Aviation Authority launched three new labor initiatives to accelerate MRO technician recruitment, development, and retention — acknowledging that workforce capacity is a binding constraint on MRO output growth.
  • Thrust Flight opens new aircraft mechanic school (March 10): Thrust Institute of Maintenance launched at Fort Worth Spinks Airport (TX), offering a 10-month FAA-certified AMT curriculum — one of several new training pipelines coming online this year to address the talent gap.
  • American Airlines donated an MD-80 APU to the Chicago Aviation Maintenance Academy (March 9) to support hands-on technician training.

Challenge: Regulatory Fragmentation

  • India–U.S. FAA/DGCA cooperation initiative (March 6): India's aviation regulator (DGCA) and the U.S. FAA announced a joint initiative with OEMs to streamline bilateral airworthiness certification and cooperation procedures — a move intended to reduce bureaucratic friction for MRO operators working across both jurisdictions.
