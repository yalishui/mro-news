Market Updates and Growth Trends

  • Aircraft MRO market on robust trajectory: BriefingWire (March 11) confirms the global MRO market continues steady growth driven by aging fleets, record load factors, and prolonged new-aircraft delivery backlogs forcing operators to extend in-service maintenance cycles. BriefingWire
  • CFM LEAP demand escalating: As Pratt & Whitney GTF-related AOG incidents begin to ease, strain is visibly shifting toward CFM LEAP-1A/1B due to emerging durability issues and rising shop-visit volumes — a fresh wave of demand through the end of the decade. Aviation Week
  • Longer shop visits normalizing: GE Aerospace and Safran (CFM International) have raised short-term shop-visit forecasts through 2028, acknowledging elevated CFM56 inductions persist longer than projected due to new-aircraft delivery delays.
  • Deloitte 2026 A&D Outlook (March 14): Aftermarket revenues continue to outpace OEM revenues as operators prioritize life extension over replacement — a structural dynamic favoring MRO providers through at least 2030. Deloitte

Regional Expansion Activities

India — Breaking News

  • Akasa Air × Noida International Airport (Jewar) MRO Partnership (March 14): India's fastest-growing carrier (fleet: ~35 aircraft, 226 on order) announced its first domestic MRO facility at NIA in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh — covering line maintenance, scheduled aircraft checks, and technical services. Addresses India's structural dependency on overseas MRO and creates high-skilled technical jobs in the NCR region. Aligns with government's push to develop domestic aviation infrastructure ahead of NIA's anticipated opening. Aviation Week

China — Regulatory Certification

  • General MRO Aerospace (GMA) receives CAAC certification (March 13): Miami-based GMA (already FAA and EASA certified) received formal authorization from China's CAAC to perform component MRO for Chinese-registered aircraft. GMA President Jonathan Cornell: "a testament to our quality systems." Positions GMA to serve Chinese operators, lessors, and MRO providers across the rapidly expanding APAC fleet. ACN Newswire

Europe — Cargo Conversion & Facility Expansion

  • EFW × APAL A330-300 P2F conversion (March 12–13): Elbe Flugzeugwerke secured a contract with Hong Kong-based Asia Pacific Aviation Leasing Group (APAL) for an Airbus A330-300 P2F conversion — EFW's first conversion deal with APAL. Reflects continued appetite for freighter conversions amid robust cargo demand and constrained new freighter deliveries. EFW AviTrader
  • EirTrade Aviation facility expansion approved: Received planning permission to expand its Knock teardown/parts-reclamation facility from 2,100 sqm to 10,500 sqm (5× increase) — construction expected to begin this year. Aviation Week

Central Asia

  • Boeing × SCAT Airlines MRO Center, Shymkent, Kazakhstan: Construction continues following the March 9 groundbreaking. The ~50,000 sqm facility — Central Asia's largest planned MRO center — will service Boeing 737, 757, 767, and 777 variants. Aviation Week

Supply Chain Developments

  • GE Aerospace invests $33M in CFM56 turbine blade manufacturing (March 12): $33M to GE's Greenville, SC facility — the world's only production site for CFM56 HPT blades — part of a broader $1 billion U.S. manufacturing investment program. ~$16.5M for advanced grinding, laser-drilling, and machining of CFM56 PIP-HPT blades; additional $200M within the same program targets LEAP aftermarket components. UK supplier ANT Industries (Atherstone) welcomed the announcement. Impact: directly targets a persistent bottleneck — HPT blade supply is the primary driver of extended CFM56 shop-visit TATs. Aviation Week
  • IATA delivery data: Aircraft deliveries stood at ~1,254 units in 2024 — roughly 30% below pre-COVID peak — keeping global backlog above 17,000 orders. This delivery shortfall is the primary structural driver of elevated MRO demand. IATA
  • DASI acquires Mesa Airlines CRJ-700/900 spare parts inventory (March 4): Large pool of in-demand regional aircraft components as Mesa transitions its fleet to Embraer E-Jets following merger with Republic Airways. Aviation Week
  • Corax (Denmark) doubles warehouse capacity (March 4): Third Copenhagen expansion completed, doubling inventory management capacity to serve European MRO demand. Aviation Week

  • HAESL (HAECO × Rolls-Royce JV) overhauled ~380 R-R engines and ~40 Trent 1000 TEN modules in 2025, representing ~25% of Rolls-Royce's global MRO network volume. Now deploying:

Major Challenges & Solutions

Hypercompetitive Feedstock Market

AerSale described the used-aircraft/engine feedstock market as "hypercompetitive" — winning only 1 in 10 bids. Scaling back planned $100M 2025 feedstock investment in 2026. Root cause: fleet retirements have slowed dramatically as airlines extend aircraft lives due to new-build delivery delays, drastically reducing the supply of teardown-ready aircraft and serviceable USM parts.

Engine Turnaround Times — Asia-Pacific

Asia-Pacific carriers continue experiencing industry-leading engine TAT delays — the top regional MRO risk. Key drivers: constrained shop capacity, spare parts scarcity, and volume of GTF-related shop visits. Responses: modular replacement strategies, accelerating USM and PMA adoption, HAESL digital toolset investment (targeting Trent engine TAT reduction).

Skilled Workforce Shortages

Singapore CAAS launched 3 new MRO workforce initiatives (March 5). Thrust Institute of Maintenance opened at Fort Worth Spinks Airport, TX — 10-month FAA-certified AMT curriculum (March 10). American Airlines donated an MD-80 APU to the Chicago Aviation Maintenance Academy for hands-on training (March 9).

Regulatory Fragmentation

India DGCA and U.S. FAA announced a joint initiative with OEMs (March 6) to streamline bilateral airworthiness certification — intended to reduce bureaucratic friction for MRO operators working across both jurisdictions.
