Supply Chain Developments

Donecle Raises €10M — Largest Drone Inspection Funding in European Aviation

  • Toulouse-based Donecle raised €10 million in a funding round led by IRDI Capital Investissement and SWEN Capital Partners, with participation from GSO Innovation and ARIS Occitanie (Donecle press release, April 15; DRONELIFE, April 16)
  • Use of funds: Accelerate international expansion (Europe + US); strengthen hiring; continue AI investment; develop new strategic partnerships
  • Platform scale: 40+ autonomous inspection drones deployed across 15+ countries; approved by leading aviation authorities and OEMs; used by commercial aviation, military aviation, business jets, and OEMs
  • Performance claim: Up to 10× faster than manual visual inspections; integrates with MRO systems to support predictive maintenance shift
  • Software vision: "Software platform that transforms collected data into actionable insights, designed to integrate with MRO systems" — positioning as an AI data platform beyond just drone hardware
  • This is the largest drone-based aviation inspection funding recorded in Europe to date

EASA Warns of Escalating Engine Parts Fraud After Container Heist

  • EASA issued a warning about fraudulent engine parts entering the supply chain after 12 shipping containers were redirected — parts believed to have been stolen mid-transit and reintroduced as legitimate inventory (Aviation Week Safety/Ops/Regulation, April 3)
  • Risk context: Engine MRO supply chain is the highest-value, highest-fraud-risk segment of the aftermarket — components worth $50,000–$500,000+ per part
  • Countermeasure trend: DUST Identity's Theseus platform (diamond-dust physical fingerprinting + AI analysis) emerging as a leading authentication solution for aerospace materials and components (Aviation Week Aircraft & Propulsion, April 15)

GA Telesis A320neo USM: LEAP-1A/PW1100G-JM Certification In Progress GA Telesis

  • GA Telesis working through certification for first LEAP-1A and PW1100G-JM components from two ex-Spirit A320neo aircraft
  • First certified USM components expected Q3 2026; trader interest described as "intense" (GA Telesis, April 15–16)
  • Boeing delivery delays + Pratt GTF situation = growing pool of completed-but-undelivered A320neo airframes entering secondary market — significant USM feedstock pipeline

OEM/Aftermarket: Material Cost Inflation Persists

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Engine MRO Turnaround Times at Crisis Levels — No Near-Term Relief

  • Narrow-body engine TAT (turnaround time): 180–200+ days — far above pre-pandemic norms of 60–90 days (Oliver Wyman, April 2026)
  • >50% of MRO operators do NOT expect TAT to improve within 3 years
  • 67% of operators report next-gen engine (LEAP/GTF) MRO costs exceeding forecasts by >21%; 25% report cost overruns exceeding 50%
  • Engine MRO = >50% of total MRO spend — a single LEAP-1A shop visit can cost $3–7M
  • Solutions emerging:
  • USM (Used Serviceable Material) adoption accelerating — GA Telesis, ATR, AJW Group positioning as primary supply
  • Modular/engine exchange programmes growing — lessors and MROs offering engine exchanges rather than slot-based shop visits
  • MRO capacity investments: Lufthansa Technik, Delta TechOps, AFI KLM E&M all expanding LEAP/GTF test cell capacity

Challenge 2: Workforce Shortage — Most Critical Long-Term Threat

  • 2/3 of MRO operators report hiring certified aircraft technicians/mechanics as "moderately to very difficult" (Oliver Wyman, April 2026)
  • >50% of operators expect workforce attrition to worsen in 2026 vs. 2025
  • US BLS projection: 5% employment growth (2024–2034); approximately 13,100 openings annually industry-wide — but certified pipeline is not keeping pace
  • Labor cost inflation: 5.5–6.0% in line/base maintenance (vs. ~3.0% pre-COVID); elevated in engine/component segment
  • Solutions scaling:
  • AI/mobile apps reducing administrative burden per technician (Korean Air/Ramco 400+ mechanics on mobile app)
  • Automation: Donecle drone inspections (10× faster than manual) reducing inspector headcount needs for visual checks
  • Apprenticeship programmes: AIESL, SIAEC, GMR/Gati Shakti partnerships expanding certified technician pipeline
  • Robotic painting, automated storage/retrieval (Korean Air ASRS integration) freeing skilled workers for higher-value tasks

Challenge 3: Supply Chain Fraud and Counterfeit Parts — EASA Warning Active

  • EASA warning (April 3, 2026): 12 containers of engine parts redirected/stolen mid-transit and reintroduced into supply chain as legitimate
  • Risk tier: Engine MRO highest-risk segment for counterfeit parts (high value, complex provenance)
  • Solutions: DUST Identity diamond-dust physical fingerprinting; blockchain part traceability (emerging commercial deployment); OEM anti-counterfeit certification programmes

Challenge 4: Tariff and Geopolitical Cost Pressure — Structural, Not Cyclical

  • 90% of MRO operators impacted by tariffs on parts procurement, repair, and engine overhaul (Oliver Wyman)
  • Geopolitical instability/currency risk ranked #2 industry disruptor for the next 5 years — up from not previously in top 5
  • Strategic responses: Regionalising supply chains (India, Southeast Asia, Central Europe MRO capacity build); increased inventory buffering; USM/PMA adoption as cost alternatives to OEM parts
