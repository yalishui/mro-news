Market Updates and Growth Trends

MRO Americas 2026 — Day 1 in Full Swing; Day 2 Underway

  • MRO Americas 2026 (April 21–23, Orange County Convention Center, Orlando, FL) — 30th anniversary edition — opened to record crowds with 17,000+ attendees, 1,000+ exhibitors, 2,000+ airline and lessor buyers from 93+ countries (Aviation Week Network, April 21)
  • Core show theme crystallized on Day 1: "Demand is not the problem; operational capacity is." Show floor conversation shifted from growth to capacity, parts availability, and cost control (Craig Shields, LinkedIn / Aviation Week Special, April 21)
  • GE Aerospace reaffirmed at its Q1 2026 earnings call (April 21) that the aftermarket remains structurally strong despite lowering full-year air departures outlook from "mid-single-digit growth" to "flat to low single-digit growth" due to Middle East conflict and macro headwinds
  • Commercial services backlog exceeds $170 billion (up ~$30 billion since 2024); total company backlog surpasses $210 billion
  • Spare parts orders up over 30% YoY since March; commercial services orders up 49% in Q1 alone
  • Full-year services revenue growth guidance raised to ~$4 billion (from previous $3.5 billion estimate)
  • LEAP service margins expected to approach overall CES service margins by 2028 — up from "breakeven a few years ago"
  • 95% of full-year spare parts revenue already in backlog — multi-year demand visibility confirmed
  • Boeing Q1 2026: Deliveries slipped to 46 aircraft in March (737 MAX production line issues); Airbus delivered 114 aircraft in Q1 (down 16% YoY) — delivery delays continue to extend legacy fleet lifespans and boost aftermarket demand

Oliver Wyman 2026 Survey Context (Carried Forward) Oliver Wyman

Regional Expansion Activities

Delta TechOps × LATAM Airlines: A320 Component Repair — Latin America Hub

  • Announced at MRO Americas (April 21): Delta TechOps and LATAM Airlines signed a long-term collaboration for Airbus A320 component repair services
  • Scope: Delta TechOps serves as sole commercial interface; repair work performed at LATAM's Sao Carlos, Brazil facility; portfolio expected to expand over time
  • Timeline: Pending Brazilian regulatory approval; expected start Q2 2026; hiring and capability ramping already underway
  • Delta TechOps ambition: Targets $1 billion revenue for the first time in 2026; $2–3 billion within a couple of years; $5 billion long-term (Marc Meredith, SVP & CCO, Delta TechOps)
  • LATAM strategic context: Reinforces ambition to establish Latin America as a "strategic hub for aviation maintenance, engineering expertise, and innovation" (Jerome Cadier, LATAM Brasil CEO)
  • Key quote: "This could evolve eventually into other fleets or other areas" — Sebastian Acuto, VP Fleet & Projects, LATAM Airlines

IAG × CFM International: Iberia Maintenance Becomes LEAP Premier MRO Shop

  • Signed April 20 (announced at MRO Americas): IAG and CFM International signed a licensing agreement making Iberia Maintenance the latest LEAP Premier MRO shop
  • License covers: Both LEAP-1A (A320neo) and LEAP-1B (737 MAX) — notably covers both engine types despite A320 dominating IAG's narrowbody fleet
  • Timeline: First LEAP engine induction planned for Q1 2027 at engine shop near Madrid-Barajas Airport (La Muñoza)
  • Scope: Initially positioned to serve European operators (including IAG member airlines), progressively expanding globally
  • Strategic significance: Iberia has maintained CFM56 since 1992; LEAP Premier membership provides access to proprietary overhaul/repair technology, training, and CFM support
  • Market context: 4,600+ LEAP engines currently operating; 8,600+ on order — LEAP fleet "increasing significantly by end of this decade" (Gaël Méheust, CFM President & CEO)
  • GE Aerospace confirms: Iberia is now GE's 7th Premier MRO partner

Ryanair Group × CFM: $800M In-House Engine MRO Plan; Morocco Under Evaluation

  • Announced at MRO Americas (April 21): Ryanair Group evaluating six potential locations for planned in-house engine MRO facilities
  • Candidates: Spain, Italy, Poland, Baltic states, Northern Ireland, Morocco
  • Decision expected within "a couple of months" on first location; two sites ultimately planned
  • Total investment: $800 million
  • GE Aerospace Q1 call (April 21) supplement: Ryanair signed agreement covering approximately 2,000 CFM56 and LEAP engines for material support and MRO services to "scale their in-house capabilities" — consistent with GE's "open MRO strategy"
  • Morocco context: Identified as one of Ryanair's fastest-growing markets; aligns with broader Africa aviation growth narrative
  • Ryanair fleet: 647 Boeing 737-800s and 737-8-200s; FY2026 target: 208 million passengers

Lufthansa Technik Canada: Calgary Facility Achieves Full Operational Readiness LHT

  • Milestone (April 16–21): LTCA successfully completed its first live LEAP-1B engine event at interim facility in Calgary, confirming full operational readiness timed to MRO Americas (Lufthansa Technik press release, April 16)
  • Current state: Interim facility fully operational with 8 maintenance bays; two training engines in shop for continuous capability building
  • Workforce: 80+ employees since February 2025 headquarters announcement; passed TCCA (Transport Canada) regulatory audit December 2025
  • Permanent facility: 150,000 sq ft at Calgary International Airport; construction start Q2 2026; will include Canada's first test cell for latest-generation aircraft engines
  • Strategic role: Part of LHT's MES (Mobile Engine Services) network; provides LEAP-1B services closer to North American customers; reduces turnaround times

Latécoère Services: Asia Expansion Planned for 2026

  • Announced at MRO Americas (April 20): Latécoère Services (500-employee aftermarket division, formed January 2025) planning to open an Asia-based entity with sales and operational activities in 2026 (Aviation Week, April 20; Thierry Dubois)
  • Model: Will replicate North America regional hub model (EWIS repair facility in Los Angeles handles demand for the region)
  • New offering: Bundled integrated engineering, production, and installation package for aircraft modifications — sold under DOA for STCs
  • Timing: Asia expansion and integrated services rollout both targeted for 2026

Supply Chain Developments

AAR Launches "Airvoyant" — Agentic AI Parts Procurement Platform

  • Announced at MRO Americas (April 21): AAR Corp. launched new subsidiary Airvoyant — an agentic AI platform for automating aviation parts procurement (Aviation Week, Lee Ann Shay)
  • Efficiency target: Reduce procurement costs by 20–30%
  • Core capabilities:
  • Multi-quote analysis: AI agents rapidly analyze supplier quotes with ranked recommendations based on historical procurement data
  • Intelligent decision making: Evaluates pricing, vendor performance history, logistics complexity
  • Vendor performance tracking: Creates data lake monitoring vendor responsiveness; rewards high-performing vendors
  • Planned: Automated vendor negotiation and optimization agents later in 2026
  • Key integrations:
  • Trax (AAR's ERP): Requisitions auto-streamed to Airvoyant when maintenance teams plan work or respond to unscheduled events
  • Aeroxchange: First third-party vendor to connect systems to Airvoyant
  • AWS: Technology backbone and architectural partner; provided financial investment
  • Strategic insight (John Holmes, AAR CEO): AAR's position as "one of the largest sellers of new and used parts" gave direct visibility into manual procurement inefficiencies

GE Aerospace: Supply Chain Stress Signals GE Aerospace

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Engine MRO Capacity — The Central Theme of MRO Americas 2026

  • Supply-demand gap widening: GE Aerospace shop visit pipeline oversubscribed by ~1/3; spare parts delinquency up 70%; narrow-body engine TAT at 180–200+ days
  • Solutions being scaled:
  • Pratt & Whitney: $100M+ across 4 U.S. sites; GTF output up 23–35% YoY
  • GE Aerospace: Open MRO strategy expanding third-party capacity; LEAP external channel now ~15% of shop visits (up from ~10% eighteen months ago); Singapore $300M investment
  • CFM/IAG: Iberia Maintenance as 7th LEAP Premier shop (Q1 2027); Ryanair 2,000-engine deal to scale in-house capabilities
  • Lufthansa Technik: Calgary interim facility fully operational; permanent 150,000 sq ft site with test cell breaking ground Q2 2026
  • Delta TechOps: First North American airline MRO licensed for both LEAP-1A and LEAP-1B; LATAM Sao Carlos collaboration adds component capacity

Challenge 2: Spare Parts Procurement Inefficiency — Now a Strategic Problem

  • Parts procurement described as the last major manual process in MRO — while inventory planning has been digitized, actual purchasing remains inefficient
  • Solutions:
  • AAR Airvoyant: Agentic AI targeting 20–30% procurement cost reduction; auto-quote analysis, vendor tracking, AWS-backed platform
  • GE Aerospace: $4B services revenue growth target; 95% spare parts revenue already in backlog; repair vs. new part economics (50% cost advantage)
  • Broader shift: Procurement speed and parts visibility elevated from back-office function to strategic capability

Challenge 3: Workforce Constraints Persist

  • Ongoing: 2/3 of operators rate certified technician hiring as "moderately to very difficult"; >50% expect attrition to worsen in 2026
  • Day 1 AMC Competition: ~90 teams from around the world competing in 6 divisions — "Olympics of aircraft maintenance" continues at MRO Americas through April 23
  • Boeing hiring: Recruiting 100+ factory workers per week; Honeywell Aerospace adding 1,200+ positions in 2026
  • Digital augmentation: AI maintenance planning (Alaska/Tailsight), drone inspection (Donecle), mobile platforms (Korean Air/Ramco) — all aimed at amplifying technician productivity

Challenge 4: Delivery Delays Sustaining Aftermarket Demand

  • Airbus Q1 2026: 114 deliveries (down 16% YoY)
  • Boeing March 2026: 46 deliveries (737 MAX line issues)
  • Impact: Every delayed new aircraft delivery = extended legacy fleet lifespan = increased maintenance burden for MROs, engine shops, and USM suppliers
  • MRO Memo insight (Aviation Week): Newer neo teardowns reflect frothy engine market — younger aircraft being parted out signals capacity desperation
