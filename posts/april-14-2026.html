Regional Expansion Activities

VAECO EASA Part 147 — Industry Response and Context

  • Following VAECO's April 9 EASA Part 147 certification, industry analysts are highlighting the broader Southeast Asian MRO training deficit (MRO Business Today, April 14):
  • Only 4 organisations in Southeast Asia now hold both EASA Part 145 and Part 147 certification simultaneously
  • Fleet growth projections for Southeast Asia (IndiGo, VietJet, AirAsia, Batik Air) imply demand for an additional 15,000–20,000 licensed maintenance engineers by 2035
  • Gap: Current Part 147 training capacity in the region cannot produce engineers at the required rate
  • Singapore, Vietnam, Indonesia, and Thailand are the primary battlegrounds for MRO training investment in the region

Corax Aviation Acquires A319 for Parts — European Narrowbody USM Adds Volume

  • Corax Aviation (European aircraft trading and teardown specialist) acquired an Airbus A319 for disassembly to expand its parts stock (Aviation Week, April 14)
  • The A319 — a shortened A320-family variant — shares the CFM56-5B engine architecture with the A320 and A321, making its teardown directly additive to the A320-family USM pool
  • Why it matters: Corax adds to the European teardown cluster alongside APOC (Toulouse), Executive Jet Support (Poland), and Tarmac Aerosave — Europe continues to be the most active teardown geography globally

Technology and Investment News

Aloft AeroArchitects STC — 737 Cabin Smart Outlet Certified

  • Aloft AeroArchitects has released a Supplemental Type Certificate (STC) for a modular, repeatable cabin interior retrofit on Boeing 737-series aircraft, featuring the first FAA-certified aviation-grade smart outlet (Spectrum Networks) for the 737 (Aviation Week, April 16)
  • The STC enables a standardised, repeatable cabin interior update that can be installed across a 737 fleet without custom engineering for each aircraft
  • Why it matters: Standardised STCs that reduce per-aircraft customisation cost are a key enabler for airline fleet-wide cabin upgrade programmes — particularly for carriers with large 737 fleets (Southwest, Ryanair, WestJet, Norwegian) managing fleet-wide interior consistency

Korean Air + Ramco Systems — Engine MRO Digitalisation

  • Korean Air has deployed Ramco Systems' Aviation Suite across its Engine Maintenance Center for maintenance, finance, and billing operations (Aviation Week, April 16; MRO Business Today, April 15)
  • 400+ mechanics and engineers are using Ramco's Mechanic Anywhere mobile app for real-time maintenance task management, documentation, and compliance reporting
  • Scope: The deployment covers the full Korean Air Engine Maintenance Center — one of Asia's largest airline-owned engine overhaul operations
  • Why it matters: Korean Air's Ramco deployment is among the largest single-site MRO ERP / mobile deployments announced this year — signalling accelerating digital transformation in Asian airline MRO
