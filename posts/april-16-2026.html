Market Updates and Growth Trends

Korean Air Fully Deploys Ramco Systems at Engine Maintenance Center

  • Korean Air has completed a full-scale deployment of Ramco Systems' Aviation Suite across its Engine Maintenance Center (EMC), integrating maintenance management, finance, and billing on a single platform (Aviation Week, April 16; MRO Business Today, April 15)
  • Scale: 400+ mechanics and engineers are actively using the Ramco Mechanic Anywhere mobile app for real-time task management, documentation, and compliance
  • Business impact: The deployment enables Korean Air's EMC to digitalise its end-to-end engine overhaul workflow — from work order generation through part traceability, regulatory compliance, and customer billing
  • Why it matters: Korean Air's EMC is one of Asia's largest independent engine overhaul operations — the Ramco deployment sets a benchmark for digital transformation in Asian airline MRO. The combination of ERP integration and mobile-first mechanic tooling is now the standard architecture for leading engine shops globally

Aloft AeroArchitects Releases 737 Cabin Interior STC — First Certified Smart Outlet

  • Aloft AeroArchitects (Boeing 737 cabin interior specialist, based in Wichita, KS) released a Supplemental Type Certificate (STC) for a modular, repeatable 737-series cabin interior retrofit featuring the first FAA-certified aviation-grade smart outlet (Spectrum Networks) for the 737 platform (Aviation Week, April 16)
  • Key innovation: The STC establishes a standardised, repeatable installation process — enabling airlines to retrofit entire 737 fleets to a consistent interior standard without custom per-aircraft engineering
  • Smart outlet significance: The Spectrum Networks aviation-grade smart outlet provides passengers with USB-A, USB-C, and AC power with surge protection — the first certified solution of its type for the 737
  • Market impact: Airlines operating large 737 fleets (Southwest ~750 aircraft, Ryanair ~600, WestJet, Norwegian) have a clear pathway to fleet-wide cabin modernisation without the historical per-aircraft engineering complexity
  • Why it matters: Interior retrofits are a major revenue source for aftermarket cabin specialists. A standardised, repeatable STC removes the primary barrier to fleet-scale deployment — expect large 737 operator RFPs to follow
