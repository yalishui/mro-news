Market Updates and Growth Trends

MRO Americas 2026 — Day 3 Concludes; Orlando Event Marks 30th Anniversary

  • MRO Americas 2026 (April 21–23, Orange County Convention Center, Orlando, FL) wrapped up its 30th anniversary edition with 17,000+ attendees, 1,000+ exhibitors, 2,000+ airline and lessor buyers from 93+ countries — the aviation aftermarket's most consequential annual business engine

  • Global MRO market: $136B in 2025 (+8% YoY); projected ~$193B by 2030 (nearly double 2019 levels)
  • Supply chain normalization: industry increasingly accepting this as a "new permanent normal" — 36+ month or never is now the dominant expectation
  • 90% of operators directly experiencing tariff impacts; geopolitical instability ranked #2 industry disruptor (up from unranked)

Commercial Aircraft MRO Market Snapshot (ePlane AI, April 23, 2026)

  • Market size $117.39B in 2025 → $143.14B by 2033 (2.51% CAGR 2026–2033)
  • Narrow-body aircraft dominates MRO landscape (highest utilization, most frequent maintenance cycles, largest global fleet); characterized by intense cost competition
  • Wide-body segment is the fastest-recovering segment (international travel resurgence, higher complexity = higher margins)
  • North America leads; Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region; Middle East & Africa emerging as new MRO hubs
  • Key drivers: air traffic growth, fleet expansion, aging aircraft requiring more maintenance, outsourcing trend, digital/predictive maintenance adoption
  • Global Aircraft Engine MRO Market facing disruption from US-Iran conflict (escalating since April 2026); supply chain trajectory fundamentally altered per DataBridge Market Research

Asia-Pacific Engine Aftermarket: 36% of Global Spending in 2026

  • Region projected to account for ~36% of overall global engine MRO spending in 2026 (Aviation Week, James Pozzi / Inside MRO, April 2)
  • Scarcity—not demand—is the defining challenge for APAC engine aftermarket sector
  • Characterized by younger, fast-growing fleets and substantial investment by OEM-aligned MRO shops
  • Hong Kong Aero Engine Services (HAESL) CEO George Edmunds: digital transformation is reshaping the business
  • Notable operators: Hainan Airlines, HK Express, Rolls-Royce ecosystem

Regional Expansion Activities

Air India × Thales: 10-Year IFE FlytCARE Deal (April 23 — Day 3 Announcement)

  • Announced via Aviation Week Rolling Daily Update (April 23): Air India signed a 10-year FlytCARE services agreement with Thales covering 57 Airbus and Boeing aircraft
  • Scope: Line maintenance, spares, repairs, and logistics management for inflight entertainment (IFE) systems
  • Service locations: Delhi and Mumbai airports — significant India domestic MRO capability build
  • Significance: First major IFE MRO commitment from Air India post-Vistara merger integration; Thales FlytCARE is a premium full-service IFE aftermarket offering

Revima × Air Astana Group: 5-Year APU Repair Agreement (April 22)

  • Announced (April 22): Auxiliary power unit (APU) and landing gear MRO specialist Revima signed a 5-year agreement with Air Astana Group for repair of 131-9A APUs
  • Scope: APUs used on Air Astana's Airbus A320 fleet; repair work at Revima's facility in Normandy, France
  • Added value: Air Astana Group covered by Revima's PrediCare predictive maintenance solution — lifecycle cost optimization and maintenance planning support
  • Significance: Central Asia's leading carrier securing long-term European APU MRO partnership; Revima expanding its APU customer base in the Middle East/Central Asia corridor

Envoy Air × Liebherr Aerospace: Embraer 175-E1 Landing Gear Overhaul (April 22)

  • Announced (April 22): Envoy Air signed a landing gear overhaul and systems maintenance agreement with Liebherr Aerospace Saline covering its Embraer 175-E1 fleet
  • Scope: Program overhauls and restorations per Embraer requirements; continuing intervals: 30,000 flight cycles or 12 calendar years
  • Significance: Envoy Air is American Eagle's regional carrier; this deal confirms Liebherr's growing Embraer landing gear MRO footprint in the Americas

DTX Aerospace × Liebherr-Aerospace: Embraer ERJ Nose Landing Gear Partnership (April 22/23)

  • Announced at MRO Americas 2026: DTX Aerospace and Liebherr-Aerospace entered a strategic partnership to service Embraer ERJ family aircraft nose landing gear components
  • Key contact: DTX Chairman Hussein Lookmanjee discussed the partnership on Aviation Week TV
  • Significance: DTX Aerospace's growing presence in the MRO sector; strategic move to capture the Embraer ERJ fleet segment; complements Liebherr's existing Embraer landing gear capabilities

Liebherr-Aerospace: Saline, Michigan Service Center — 5th Expansion Since 1993

  • Highlighted at MRO Americas 2026 (Booth #4552): Liebherr showcased its expanded service center in Saline, Michigan — its fifth expansion since 1993
  • New workshop inaugurated October 2025: Designed for full MRO activities including:
  • Test, repair, overhaul, and recore capacity for Liebherr heat transfer equipment
  • Supported platforms: Airbus, Boeing, Bombardier, Embraer, and Mitsubishi
  • Increased landing gear processing capacity at the main facility
  • Expanded MRO activities for pneumatic components
  • New exclusive partnership: LHColus (Brazilian engineering specialist) — Liebherr-Aerospace Saline named exclusive international distributor of the Class F Quick Change cargo conversion kit (quick, fully reversible passenger-to-freighter conversion)

Supply Chain Developments

Oliver Wyman: Supply Chain "New Permanent Normal" — No Recovery in Sight Oliver Wyman

  • Key finding: Industry answers on supply chain normalization increasingly shifted to "never normalizes" or "36+ months" — structurally embedded high costs and shortages must now be permanently planned for
  • Material cost inflation: 2025 actual exceeded forecast by 100–200 basis points — driven by materials scarcity, geopolitical instability, spare parts inventory buildup, aging fleet repair demand, and new technology development costs flowing into aftermarket pricing
  • Spare parts inventory strategy shift: Airlines and MROs building higher buffer stocks as just-in-time delivery becomes unreliable; this in turn sustains demand for USM (Used Serviceable Material) and PMA parts
  • GE Aerospace Q1 data (from April 22 summary context): Spare parts delinquency up ~70% since 2024; 95% of full-year spare parts revenue already in backlog; commercial services orders up 49% in Q1 alone — supply chain stress continuing into Q2 2026

Boeing 787-9 Gear Exchange Program: Ramping Up (Aviation Week, Sean Broderick) Aviation Week

  • Announced via MRO Americas 2026 coverage: Boeing is ramping up its 787-9 landing gear exchange program ahead of increasing demand over the next several years
  • Market context: Boeing preparing to capture significant share in an increasingly competitive 787-9 gear exchange market
  • Significance: Wide-body aftermarket segment (specifically heavy maintenance) is in recovery — operators returning previously grounded post-pandemic fleets to service; Boeing positioning to monetize OEM-backed gear exchange as a premium aftermarket offering

Sanad Achieves Record 2025 Revenue (Aviation Week, James Pozzi) Aviation Week

  • MRO Americas 2026 coverage: Engine MRO and asset management specialist Sanad achieved record revenue in 2025 — driven by global engine shop capacity constraints that pushed engine inductions higher
  • Context: As engine shops globally remain oversubscribed (GE Aerospace shop visit pipeline oversubscribed by ~1/3), third-party independent engine MROs like Sanad benefit from the demand that exceeds OEM/OEM-aligned shop capacity
  • CEO featured: Aviation Week TV interviewed Sanad's Managing Director & Group CEO on growth plans

Technology and Investment News

GE Aerospace: Multi-Region MRO and Manufacturing Workforce Investment GE Aerospace

  • Announced at MRO Americas 2026 (Aviation Week, Lindsay Bjerregaard): GE Aerospace is investing millions of dollars across the U.S., Europe, and Asia to support MRO and manufacturing workforce cultivation and development
  • Scope: Workforce development programs, skills training, and capacity building to address chronic technician shortages across GE's global MRO and manufacturing footprint
  • Strategic alignment: Part of GE Aerospace's broader push to scale external MRO capacity (LEAP external channel now ~15% of shop visits) and address the 66% of operators reporting "moderate to very difficult" technician hiring challenges
  • Flight Deck digital initiatives in parallel:
  • McAllen, TX: LEAP HPT repair time reduced >50% via cell redesign
  • Lafayette, IN: AI-based material assistant predicts LEAP work scopes 9 months in advance
  • Singapore: $300M investment underway at repair facility

AI Adoption: 58% Still in "Experimentation" — But Value Proof Emerging

  • Oliver Wyman 2026 Survey: AI adoption landscape shows a paradox
  • 58% of MRO operators remain in "experimentation" phase (unchanged year-over-year — no net progress)
  • 66% of those experimenting are meeting or exceeding expectations — up significantly from prior years
  • Interpretation: Better understanding of AI's strengths and weaknesses is helping the remaining 42% deploy more targeted use cases
  • Core barriers to broader deployment:
  • Data quality and availability — the #1 obstacle
  • Only 1/3 of operators planning to invest in IT infrastructure (needed to support AI)
  • 50% planning to invest in AI itself — but without proportional IT infrastructure investment, these projects will likely remain stuck in experimentation
  • AI technology spotlight at MRO Americas 2026:
  • AAR Airvoyant: Agentic AI parts procurement (see April 22 summary)
  • Alaska Airlines × Tailsight: AI maintenance planning (first major airline deployment)
  • GE Aerospace Flight Deck: AI material assistant, digital cell redesign
  • Donecle: Drone AI inspection (€10M funding, April 15)

Oliver Wyman: New-Generation Engine Cost Overruns Are Severe Oliver Wyman

  • 66% of operators report next-gen narrow-body engine maintenance costs exceeding expectations by more than 21%
  • 25% report costs exceeding expectations by more than 50%
  • Context: GTF durability recovery ongoing but not yet complete; LEAP durability concerns emerging as strain shifts from GTF (GE/Safran raised shop-visit forecasts through 2028)
  • Implication: MRO pricing models built on OEM assumptions need fundamental revision; operators and lessors increasingly scrutinizing engine MRO contracts for cost-overrun protections

JetBlue Selects Skywise Fleet Performance+ Across Full Fleet

  • Announced via Aviation Week (Christine Boynton): JetBlue agreed to deploy Skywise Fleet Performance+ (S.FP+) across its entire Airbus A320 family and A220 fleet
  • Scope: Fleet-wide predictive maintenance and performance optimization platform
  • Significance: JetBlue joins a growing list of carriers standardizing on Airbus/Skywise as the operational data platform of record; the A220 fleet addition is notable as the platform's relatively young installed base presents a clean digital MRO entry point

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Chronic Workforce Shortage — No Near-Term Solution

  • Oliver Wyman 2026 Survey:
  • 66% of operators rate certified technician hiring as "moderately to very difficult"
  • >50% expect attrition to worsen in 2026
  • U.S. certified mechanic supply failing to keep pace with demand growth
  • Labor cost inflation: 5.5–6.0% in 2025 (航线维修/机身维修), compared to ~3.0% pre-COVID baseline
  • Solutions in scale:
  • LAUNCH Technical Workforce Solutions (announced April 23 at MRO Americas): expanding its Global Access Program — connecting operators with 500+ deployed technicians across 79 countries; opened a new Modification & Training Center of Excellence; acquired JMC Aviation (expanding mobile repair teams and Part 145 modification capabilities)
  • Oliver Wyman × IAWA "Lift Off To Leadership" study: research into women in aviation challenges launched at MRO Americas — diversifying the talent pipeline is now a strategic priority
  • AMC Competition at MRO Americas: ~90 teams from around the world competing in 6 divisions through April 23 — celebrating the profession and inspiring next-generation talent
  • GE Aerospace workforce investments: Millions of dollars across U.S., Europe, Asia for MRO and manufacturing workforce development

Challenge 2: Supply Chain Structural Fragility — Now Accepted as Permanent

  • Oliver Wyman finding: Industry is increasingly accepting supply chain constraints as a structural "new normal" rather than a temporary disruption
  • Solutions:
  • AAR Airvoyant: AI-powered procurement targeting 20–30% cost reduction; addresses the "last manual process" in MRO
  • USM/PMA adoption: Repaired parts costing 50% less than new parts; USM market growing as operators seek cost relief
  • Spare parts inventory buildup: Rational response to delivery delays and supply chain unreliability — but increases working capital requirements
  • Boeing 787-9 gear exchange program: OEM-backed exchange reducing operator exposure to long repair queues

Challenge 3: Geopolitical Disruption — New #2 Industry Risk Factor

  • Oliver Wyman 2026: Geopolitical instability / tariffs ranked #2 industry disruptor (new entry, instantly rose to second place)
  • 90% of operators experiencing direct tariff impacts — especially in parts procurement, repairs, and engine overhauls
  • US-Iran conflict impact: Global Aircraft Engine MRO market trajectory fundamentally altered per DataBridge Market Research; Middle East route changes redirecting some maintenance demand
  • No retirement uptick despite high oil prices (Aviation Week, Sean Broderick, April 21): Despite fuel cost pressures, no evidence of accelerated fleet retirement — meaning older, maintenance-intensive aircraft remain in service longer, sustaining MRO demand

Challenge 4: Delivery Delays Sustaining Aftermarket Demand — Double-Edged Sword

  • Airbus Q1 2026: 114 deliveries (down 16% YoY); hundreds of completed aircraft sitting without engines (GTF/LEAP shortages)
  • Boeing March 2026: 46 deliveries (737 MAX line issues ongoing)
  • Upside: Delayed deliveries extend legacy fleet lifespan, boost USM demand, sustain MRO shop loading
  • Downside: New aircraft entering service creates fresh MRO demand; delivery delays eventually normalize, creating future demand volatility risk
