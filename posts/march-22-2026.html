Regional Expansion Activities

FAA Launches Bilateral MRO Mutual Recognition Rulemaking

  • FAA (Sean Broderick, Aviation Week, March 19) initiated a formal rulemaking to broaden mutual recognition of MRO approvals across bilateral aviation safety agreements (BASA)
  • Background: Under current U.S. law, FAA must separately approve each non-U.S. repair station even when a BASA already exists — duplicating oversight without safety benefit; Canada is the sole current exception under FAR Part 43
  • Proposed rule is "permissive": Would allow (not require) FAA to enter mutual recognition arrangements with qualified BASA partners such as EU member states
  • Regulatory milestone: Rule has passed rulemaking committee review, been formally accepted, and received a Regulatory Identification Number (RIN) — now an official regulatory action
  • ARSA-led industry coalition had petitioned FAA in 2020 to amend FAR Part 43 to enable this change, citing cost savings and reallocation of oversight resources to operational safety
  • FAA Flight Standards General Aviation Group manager Chris Parfitt confirmed the rulemaking at ARSA's annual conference

Source: Aviation Week, Sean Broderick, March 19, 2026

IATP S-Pool: New Chair Targets Boeing 787 AOG Readiness

  • David Kerr (Head of Technical Procurement & Supply, Norwegian Air) appointed as new chair of IATP S-Pool, which covers parts pooling for B757, B767, and B787 fleets (Keith Mwanalushi, Aviation Week, March 17)
  • Primary mission: Improve B787 AOG readiness across IATP member airlines
  • Key challenges Kerr identified:
  • 787 parts demand is difficult to predict — insufficient historical data, inconsistent demand patterns across operators
  • High-value components with long repair cycles create liquidity risk
  • Part number complexity: Airline part numbers often don't map directly to IATP-tracked component numbers, creating AOG matching errors
  • Strategies in play:
  • Data-driven analysis using Boeing, Norwegian Air, and member airline demand patterns to proactively identify inventory gaps
  • Technical access verification — confirming part applicability and documentation before AOG events occur
  • Growing S-Pool 787 inventory contributions from airlines and suppliers
  • New member integration: Riyadh Air recently joined IATP, adding regional coverage
  • Value proof: IATP data shows adding 787 components "significantly increased" pool value vs. traditional power-by-the-hour contracts

Source: Aviation Week, Keith Mwanalushi, March 17, 2026

Technology and Investment News

MRO Hangar Heating: Energy Efficiency Innovation Accelerates

  • U.S. winter storms in 2026 have forced MROs to rethink hangar heating strategies, accelerating adoption of energy-efficient solutions (Keith Mwanalushi, Aviation Week, March 19)
  • Problem with traditional forced-air systems:
  • Thermal stratification — warm air pools at hangar ceiling, not the work zone
  • Rapid heat loss every time hangar doors open for aircraft movement
  • Ground-level cold spots causing discomfort, condensation on aircraft surfaces
  • Solution: Infrared radiant heating systems
  • Heats personnel, objects, and structures directly (not air) — analogous to solar radiation
  • Key advantage: Temperature recovers immediately after hangar doors reopen — no full space re-heat required
  • Enables zone-specific heating — target individual work areas rather than the entire hangar volume
  • More stable working conditions under high air exchange rates
  • Implementation considerations: Ceiling clearance, structural load capacity, phased installation to minimize operational disruption, possible electrical upgrade requirements
  • Industry drivers: Rising energy costs + sustainability/ESG pressures on MRO facilities; workforce comfort and safety

Source: Aviation Week, Keith Mwanalushi, March 19, 2026
