AAR Airvoyant: Agentic AI Parts Procurement — Day 1 Launch (Preview)

  • AAR Corp staging launch of Airvoyant at MRO Americas Day 1 (April 21) — an AI-driven, fully integrated aviation parts procurement platform
  • Key features: automated workflow agents, real-time supplier inventory comparison, Aeroxchange and Trax ERP integration
  • Cost reduction target: 20–30% on parts procurement — addresses the #1 operational cost variable for independent MROs
  • Platform compares supplier quotes, history, and logistics complexity autonomously
  • Represents the first agentic AI platform commercialised at scale for aviation parts sourcing

  • Targeting a larger share of the competitive widebody overhaul market

Major Challenges and Solutions

Engine TAT Crisis — Pre-Show Assessment

  • Narrow-body engine TAT: 180–200+ days vs. 60–90 day pre-pandemic norms; no near-term structural improvement expected
  • 67% of MRO operators report LEAP/GTF shop visit costs exceeding forecasts; 25% report overruns >50%
  • GTF shop visits stretching toward ~300 days in worst-case scenarios (Oliver Wyman)
  • P&W's $100M+ U.S. investment is a direct response — but capacity additions won't provide relief until 2027–2028

Tariff and Geopolitical Cost Pressure — Pre-Show Poll

  • 90% of MRO operators impacted by tariffs on parts procurement, repair, overhaul
  • Geopolitical instability ranked #2 industry disruptor for 2026–2031 — new entrant to top-5 list
  • Regionalisation responses: India, Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Singapore), Central Europe MRO capacity investments all accelerating as operators seek supply chain resilience
