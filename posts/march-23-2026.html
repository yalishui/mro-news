Market Overview

The global aviation MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul) market continues to experience robust growth, valued at approximately $98.38 billion in 2026 and projected to reach $155.02 billion by 2035, representing a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5.19%. The industry is navigating a complex landscape characterized by aging fleets, supply chain constraints, and transformative digital technologies.

1. Juneyao and Lufthansa Technik Seal Landmark Engine Services Deal LHT

Juneyao Group, one of China's leading aviation companies, and Lufthansa Technik have entered into an exclusive, landmark agreement for comprehensive engine overhaul services. This strategic partnership marks a significant expansion of Lufthansa Technik's presence in the Asia-Pacific region and underscores the growing demand for high-quality MRO services in China's rapidly expanding aviation market.

Key Implications:

  • Strengthens cross-border MRO collaboration between Europe and Asia
  • Addresses growing maintenance needs for Juneyao's expanding fleet
  • Signals continued consolidation and strategic partnerships in the MRO sector

2. Oliver Wyman Releases 26th Annual Global Fleet and MRO Market Forecast Oliver Wyman

Oliver Wyman has published its "Global Fleet and MRO Market Forecast 2026-2036," the firm's 26th annual assessment of the 10-year outlook for the commercial airline fleet and MRO industry. The report highlights how aviation is balancing record passenger demand with aging fleets and ongoing supply-chain challenges.

Key Findings:

  • Record low aircraft retirements have resulted from insufficient new aircraft inventory
  • Airlines are operating older aircraft, impacting aftermarket MRO costs
  • Supply chain constraints remain elevated, with engine shop visit timelines stretching
  • Fleet utilization has reached record levels, intensifying maintenance pressure

3. US Civil Aviation Maintenance Industry Shows Strong Employment Growth

As of the start of 2026, the US civil aviation maintenance industry employed nearly 346,000 workers and generated $78.2 billion in economic activity. Overall employment showed almost 2% growth, reflecting the industry's resilience and ongoing demand for skilled technical personnel despite broader workforce challenges.

Fleet and Market Insights

Aircraft Delivery Challenges Persist

According to industry reports, January 2026 showed Boeing delivering 46 commercial aircraft compared with 19 deliveries for Airbus. However, both manufacturers continue to face significant delivery shortfalls, with backlogs exceeding 17,000 orders globally. The production constraints are contributing to:

  • Extended aircraft lifespans and increased heavy maintenance requirements
  • Higher aftermarket costs for spare parts and component overhauls
  • Accelerated adoption of digital maintenance technologies to optimize efficiency

Market Concentration Trend

In a notable development, market analysis reveals that just 35 MRO providers are projected to account for approximately half of all observed heavy maintenance events in 2025, indicating increasing market consolidation and specialization within the industry.

Technology and Innovation

Digital Transformation Accelerates

The MRO industry is embracing digital technologies to address operational challenges:

  • AI-driven Digital Twins: Gaining traction for predictive maintenance planning
  • Autonomous Inspection Systems: Increasing deployment for enhanced efficiency
  • Advanced Analytics: Improving parts availability and reducing turnaround times
  • Predictive Maintenance: Shifting from reactive to proactive maintenance strategies

Industry experts highlight 2026 as a pivotal year for digital twin adoption in aviation maintenance, with AI, simulation, and predictive planning transforming traditional MRO operations.

Engine OEMs Enhance MRO Capabilities

Major engine manufacturers are making significant strides in MRO support:

  • Pratt & Whitney, Rolls-Royce, GE Aerospace, and CFM International are rolling out new engine durability upgrades designed to significantly improve time-on-wing performance
  • OEMs are expanding their MRO networks to meet growing demand
  • Service agreements between airlines and engine manufacturers are being renewed and expanded

Regional Developments

Asia-Pacific Market Expands

The Asia-Pacific region continues to demonstrate strong growth in the MRO sector:

  • MRO Asia-Pacific 2026 conference scheduled as the largest and most influential aviation aftermarket exhibition in the region
  • MRO Guangzhou continues as the largest MRO exhibition in Asia, focusing on commercial air transport maintenance
  • The Asia-Pacific aircraft engine MRO market shows substantial growth driven by surging air travel demand and expanding aircraft fleets

European and North American Markets

  • Air Nostrum Engineering and Maintenance has renewed and expanded its component support agreement with Fokker Services Group (FSG), covering its growing fleet
  • Strategic partnerships between European and Asian MRO providers are increasing
  • North American MRO providers continue to consolidate and expand service offerings

The aviation supply chain landscape in 2026 shows several key trends:

1. Elevated Backlogs: Aircraft order backlogs remain above 17,000 units globally

2. Engine/MRO Constraints: Shop visit timelines continue to stretch, creating capacity challenges

3. Spare Parts Lead Times: Extended delivery times for critical components

4. Supply Chain Resilience: Airlines and MRO providers are investing in inventory management and supplier diversification
