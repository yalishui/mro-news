Market Updates and Growth Trends

Oliver Wyman / ARSA 2026–2036 Global Fleet & MRO Market Forecast: Demand Stronger Than Ever, Market Structurally Strained Oliver Wyman

  • The Aeronautical Repair Station Association (ARSA) and Oliver Wyman Vector released the 2026 Global Fleet & MRO Market Forecast (26th annual edition) in mid-March — the most closely watched annual benchmark in the industry
  • Headline outlook: Global commercial MRO spend is forecast to grow strongly through 2036, driven by record passenger demand, an aging in-service fleet, and persistently delayed OEM deliveries that force airlines to extend the life of existing aircraft
  • Key structural themes flagged by Oliver Wyman:
  • "Supply chain throttling" remains the dominant constraint — not demand softness — with shop visit queues, parts lead times, and certified technician shortages all acting as simultaneous bottlenecks
  • GTF (Pratt & Whitney PW1100G) and LEAP engine MRO remain the most acute pressure points: average shop visit turnaround times for GTF engines have stretched from ~60 days (pre-pandemic baseline) to ~300 days in 2026, according to independent industry analysis
  • Engine MRO is forecast to be the fastest-growing sub-segment, driven by the maturing narrowbody fleets and continued in-service issues with next-generation powerplants
  • Airframe MRO demand supported by extended aircraft retirement ages as OEM delivery deferrals push airlines to operate older widebodies and narrowbodies beyond planned retirement
  • Component and avionics MRO growing steadily, with increased demand for line-replaceable unit (LRU) repair driven by avionics modernisation mandates
  • Fleet forecast highlights:
  • Global commercial fleet expected to cross 35,000 aircraft by 2030, versus ~27,000 today
  • Aircraft delivery backlogs exceeding 17,000 units globally continue to sustain demand for maintenance of in-service fleets
  • Narrowbody production shortfalls: OEM delivery shortfalls estimated at 5,300+ units versus scheduled deliveries through 2025–2026
  • ARSA comment: The 2026 report describes the maintenance community's "continued resilience in the face of unprecedented supply chain pressures" — acknowledging the industry is simultaneously absorbing record volumes of shop visits while navigating parts shortages and labour constraints

Source: ARSA / Oliver Wyman Global Fleet & MRO Market Forecast 2026–2036, released March 16, 2026; ATEC-AMT, March 25, 2026

Manufacturing and Supply Chain Investment

Safran Establishes €125M Wallonia Compressor Components Subsidiary — Strategic Supply Chain Reinforcement Safran

  • Safran Aero Boosters announced on March 27, 2026 the creation of a new subsidiary in Wallonia, Belgium, to manufacture aircraft engine compressor components — representing an investment of €125 million in partnership with Belgian and Walloon public authorities
  • What is being built:
  • A dedicated industrial site producing low-pressure compressor (LPC) blades and structural components for aircraft engines, including next-generation powerplants
  • Safran Aero Boosters is the primary supplier of LPC modules for CFM International's LEAP engine (used on A320neo and 737 MAX), making this investment a direct capacity reinforcement for the world's best-selling engine programme
  • The new plant will be operated as a joint-venture subsidiary, leveraging Wallonia's existing aerospace industrial ecosystem (including SONACA, Thales Alenia Space Belgium, and Safran's existing Liège/Milmort operations)
  • Strategic rationale:
  • LEAP engine deliveries accelerating — CFM International targeting 2,000+ LEAP deliveries per year in 2026–2027, requiring a step-change in LPC component supply
  • Safran Aero Boosters' existing Belgian facilities running at near-capacity; new dedicated subsidiary protects the supply chain while preserving quality and IP within the Safran ecosystem
  • Public partnership with Walloon regional government provides co-investment support, reflecting the region's commitment to aerospace manufacturing competitiveness
  • Key figures:
  • Investment: €125 million (total project cost including facility, equipment, and ramp-up)
  • New jobs: Several hundred expected over the programme ramp-up period (Wallonian government statement)
  • Timeline: Construction and qualification expected to reach production readiness by 2027–2028
  • MRO supply chain significance: More LPC components in the supply chain directly reduces lead times for LEAP engine shop visits — helping to ease the chronic parts shortages that are currently extending GTF/LEAP MRO turnaround times to record levels

Source: Safran Group press release, March 27, 2026; AviTrader, March 30, 2026; MRO Business Today, March 28, 2026

Regional Aviation and MRO Development

India–EU Aviation Production Pact: Tata-Airbus H125 Assembly Line Signals India's Manufacturing Ambitions

  • India and the European Union signed a Working Arrangement on Industrial Aviation Production on March 23, 2026 (published by the EU on March 27), formalising a framework agreement between India's DGCA and EASA to harmonise aviation safety and production standards
  • Tata-Airbus milestone:
  • The Tata-Airbus H125 helicopter assembly line in Karnataka (Tumakuru facility) is confirmed to become operational in April 2026 — the first commercial helicopter assembly line in India, producing Airbus H125 single-engine turbine helicopters under the Make in India programme
  • The H125 assembly marks a milestone in transitioning India from an aviation services importer to a manufacturing participant
  • MRO policy implications:
  • The India-EU production pact establishes a bilateral regulatory framework intended to streamline maintenance approvals, component certifications, and airworthiness validation between Indian and European authorities
  • This directly addresses a long-standing friction point for Indian MRO operators seeking EASA recognition for maintenance work performed on European-registered or European-operated aircraft
  • DGCA–EASA alignment is expected to enable more Indian MRO providers to seek dual approvals, opening the door to regional MRO export services
  • India aircraft supply bottleneck context:
  • India's Civil Aviation Minister K. Rammohan Naidu publicly acknowledged on March 30 that while 1,700 new aircraft are on order, supply chain constraints mean deliveries cannot keep pace with demand — forcing carriers like IndiGo, Air India, and SpiceJet to extend the operational life of existing fleets
  • This directly translates to elevated domestic MRO demand: older aircraft need more frequent and complex maintenance, and the shortage of new aircraft means MRO is even more operationally critical than usual
  • MRO Business Today estimated that India's fleet-growth story is entering a "maintenance intensity" phase — where the gap between fleet ambition and actual deliveries sustains an outsized, multi-year MRO demand pipeline

Source: Aviation Today, LiveMint, The Hindu Business Line, MRO Business Today, March 27–30, 2026

Industry Outlook

Closing Q1, Opening Q2: March 31, 2026 marks the end of the first quarter — and the MRO industry is entering Q2 in arguably the strongest demand environment in its history, while simultaneously facing the most structurally constrained supply picture in at least a decade.

The Oliver Wyman / ARSA forecast crystallises the core paradox: global MRO spend will be larger than ever over the next decade, but the industry's ability to deliver that maintenance — on time, at quality, at scale — remains fundamentally limited by parts lead times, certified technician shortages, and OEM component supply constraints. Safran's €125 million Belgium investment is a direct, high-conviction bet on solving one piece of that puzzle for LEAP engine LPC components. It won't be the last such investment announced in 2026.

Meanwhile, India's combination of a government-backed MRO hub strategy, a new DGCA-EASA production pact, and the imminent Tata-Airbus assembly line launch signals that the country's long-anticipated rise as a major MRO destination is moving from aspiration to execution. With 1,700+ aircraft on order and supply chain constraints forcing extended fleet operations, India's domestic MRO demand alone justifies the investment wave underway.

All eyes now turn to MRO Americas (April 21–23, Orlando) — where the industry will spend three days processing Q1's themes and signalling its Q2 priorities. The 30th anniversary show is expected to be the largest in the event's history.
