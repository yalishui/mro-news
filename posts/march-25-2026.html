Market Updates and Growth Trends

IBA: Aircraft Engine Values Remain Elevated — Demand Continues to Outpace Supply

  • IBA (aviation market intelligence) confirmed in its latest weekly update that engine values across most segments remain at historically elevated levels, with MRO backlogs and supply chain constraints expected to persist well into 2026–2027 (IBA Aero / Aerospace Innovations, March 25)
  • GTF / LEAP-1A/1B outlook: AOG events continue to run above historical norms; demand for serviceable spare engines and quick-turn shop slots remains strong
  • Widebody market: 777-300ER full-life engine values holding above $32 million; A330ceo (Trent 700) post-overhaul values climbing as operators extend legacy fleets
  • IBA forward view: No near-term easing expected — elevated demand, constrained shop capacity, and delayed new aircraft deliveries form a self-reinforcing cycle keeping both values and lease rates elevated

Source: IBA Aero / Aerospace Innovations, March 25, 2026

Aviation MRO Market Reaches $61.5 Billion Projected by 2033

  • A new market research report published March 23–25, 2026 projects the global Aircraft Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul (MRO) market to reach $61.5 billion by 2033 (DataInsights Market, March 2026)
  • Drivers include: rising passenger air travel, aging global aircraft fleets, increasing regulatory complexity, and accelerating adoption of digital/predictive maintenance
  • Fastest-growing segments: Airframe heavy checks, line maintenance, and engine MRO (largest single segment at ~42% of total spend)
  • Geographic growth leaders: Asia-Pacific (driven by fleet expansion), Middle East (infrastructure investment), and North America (persistent MRO demand from large legacy fleets)

Source: DataInsights Market Research, March 2026

Regional Expansion Activities

Magnetic Engines Completes Second LPT Major Module Replacement — Verona, Italy Aviation Week

  • Magnetic Engines (engine MRO subsidiary of Magnetic Group / Direct Maintenance) announced the successful completion of its second Low Pressure Turbine (LPT) major module on-wing replacement at its Verona, Italy facility (Aviation Week, March 25)
  • The first LPT module replacement was completed in December 2025 in Varna, Bulgaria, demonstrating Magnetic Engines' growing capability for complex on-wing CFM56-5B engine repairs across its European network
  • Significance: On-wing LPT module replacement is technically demanding — reducing aircraft downtime versus traditional shop visits; milestone expands Magnetic Engines' capability catalog for European airline customers

Source: Aviation Week Network, March 25, 2026

Direct Maintenance (Magnetic Line) Wins American Airlines Wide-Body Support Contract at Dublin Aviation Week

  • Direct Maintenance, operating under the Magnetic Line brand, secured a new contract with American Airlines to provide wide-body aircraft maintenance support at Dublin Airport, Ireland (MRO Business Today, March 25)
  • Adds to Dublin's growing role as a European maintenance hub for transatlantic carriers
  • Part of the Magnetic Group's commercial expansion strategy in Western Europe — building long-term relationships with major North American and European network airlines

Source: MRO Business Today, March 25, 2026

GE Aerospace Commits €110M+ to European MRO and Manufacturing Expansion GE Aerospace

  • GE Aerospace announced plans to invest more than €110 million in its European manufacturing and engine MRO network to expand capacity and workforce capabilities (MRO Business Today, March 25)
  • Investment targets improved LEAP engine shop visit throughput as first-cycle overhaul volumes continue to ramp up through 2026–2027
  • Part of a global OEM-led MRO capacity investment cycle: GE Aerospace (Europe), Safran (Hyderabad €200M), MTU (Fort Worth), and others responding to structural engine MRO capacity crunch
  • Reflects OEM confidence in sustained, multi-year elevated MRO demand — engine backlogs not expected to normalize until 2028 at earliest

Source: MRO Business Today, March 25, 2026

Technology and Test Equipment News

ATEQ Aviation Acquires T-RX Avionics Tester from CCX Technologies — Streamlining MRO Compliance Workflows

  • ATEQ Aviation acquired the T-RX compact avionics tester from CCX Technologies, with the deal announced March 25, 2026 (Aviation Week, March 25)
  • Strategic value: Combining T-RX with ATEQ's pneumatic data test equipment allows MRO shops to perform complete FAA 14 CFR 91.411 and 91.413 compliance testing — altimeter/transponder certifications — in a single, consolidated workflow
  • Reduces test equipment footprint, technician time, and calibration overhead for operators maintaining large transponder/altimeter-equipped fleets
  • ATEQ Aviation is a specialist in aviation test and measurement equipment; the T-RX acquisition expands their avionics compliance product line

Source: Aviation Week Network, March 25, 2026

Safran Electrical & Power Expands Sarasota MRO Facility — U.S. Electrical Systems Capacity Boost Safran

  • Safran Electrical & Power significantly expanded its Sarasota, Florida facility to strengthen its U.S. MRO capabilities for aircraft electrical and power systems (MRO Business Today, March 25)
  • The expansion adds shop floor area, testing capacity, and certified technician positions
  • Part of Safran Group's broader strategy to localize MRO capability near its largest customer bases in North America, reducing AOG recovery time for U.S.-based operators
  • Complements Safran's major Hyderabad engine MRO investment (€200M) — showing the group investing simultaneously in component and engine MRO on multiple continents

Source: MRO Business Today, March 25, 2026

Industry Outlook

March 25 consolidates two accelerating themes from this week:

1. OEM-led MRO capacity investment is accelerating. GE Aerospace's €110M+ European commitment — announced back-to-back with Safran Sarasota, FL Technics Punta Cana, and Lufthansa Technik Dubai — signals that OEMs and independent MROs alike have concluded elevated MRO demand is structural, not cyclical. These multi-year capital commitments lock in capacity that won't come online until 2027–2028, reinforcing the view that the current supply crunch will persist.

2. On-wing and mobile repair capabilities are gaining traction. Magnetic Engines' second LPT major module on-wing replacement — this time in Verona after December's Varna milestone — demonstrates a scalable model for reducing aircraft downtime through complex mobile/on-wing interventions. As airlines face extended MRO turnaround times, on-wing heavy work represents a meaningful AOG-reduction lever. Watch for more MROs to develop and advertise these capabilities through 2026.
