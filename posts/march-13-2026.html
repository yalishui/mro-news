Major Challenges & Solutions

1. Skilled Labor Shortage (Top Priority)

Severe shortage of qualified technicians, engineers, and planners — especially in North America and the Middle East. Experienced mechanics retiring faster than new entrants join. Growing need for expertise in next-gen aircraft and composite materials. Solutions: partnerships with technical schools, competitive compensation, clear career pathways, AR/VR training platforms.

2. Rising Costs

Labor cost increases outpacing airline revenue growth; material cost escalation; capacity investment requirements; geopolitical factors driving tariff fluctuations. Solutions: productivity improvements via technology adoption, predictive maintenance reducing unplanned expenses, long-term capacity and pricing partnerships.

3. Supply Chain Instability

Extended delivery cycles for specific components; geopolitical risks; parts availability constraints affecting TATs. Solutions: proactive planning and inventory buffers, higher component repair rates, enhanced supplier collaboration, USM market utilization, local supply chain investment.

4. Integration and Transformation Pressures

Major airline mergers (e.g., Korean Air with Asiana) creating complex facility, team, and systems integration challenges. Solutions: phased integration planning, process standardization, change management programs, technology platform consolidation.

5. Sustainability

Growing pressure to adopt green maintenance practices, SAF compatibility requirements, waste reduction programs, and energy-efficient facilities. Environmental regulations (EU REACH) adding compliance costs.
