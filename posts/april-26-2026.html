Market Updates and Growth Trends

Jet Fuel Crisis Deepens — Aviation's Sharpest Shock in Over a Decade

  • Late-February 2026 trigger: Middle East conflict escalation + Strait of Hormuz supply disruptions
  • February 2026 average U.S. airline jet fuel cost: $2.39/gallon (BTS)
  • Mid-April 2026 composite spot prices: >$4.78/gallon (BTS, S&P Global Platts, IATA)
  • Mid-April 2026 retail FBO prices: >$8.60/gallon
  • Fuel has more than doubled since February 2026
  • Fuel share of operating costs: Jumped from 25–30% historical norm to dominant expense line

MRO Demand Bifurcation Under Fuel Shock

  • 2025 global MRO market: $136B (+8% YoY) — Oliver Wyman
  • 2030 projection: ~$193B — Oliver Wyman
  • 2026 MRO demand base-case trim: 4–7% (line maintenance and legacy-engine shop visits see steepest near-term contraction)
  • Rising MRO segments: Heavy checks pulled forward (fuel-saving modifications), engine performance restoration, condition-based/predictive maintenance, SAF compatibility testing
  • Declining MRO segments: Line maintenance (lower utilization), routine engine shop visits, legacy-engine shop visits

Ryanair In-House Engine MRO — Morocco Under Consideration (April 22, reported April 26)

  • Context: At MRO Americas 2026 (April 21), Ryanair announced an $800M in-house engine MRO plan across 6 locations — the largest airline self-sufficiency push in MRO history
  • Investment scale: ~€500M for building dedicated engine service centers
  • Significance: Morocco positioning itself as an African/Mediterranean MRO hub; Ryanair's move toward engine self-sufficiency signals a potential paradigm shift in how large LCCs approach maintenance

Technology and Investment News

Engine Performance Technology Prioritized Under Fuel Crisis

  • Each 1% improvement in specific fuel consumption = millions of dollars in savings at current price levels
  • CFM56 and V2500 engine washes being advanced by 300–500 flight hours (previously discretionary, now economically urgent)
  • Fuel-saving modifications installed during accelerated downtime: engine performance restoration, drag-reduction kits, deep engine washes
  • Airlines pulling maintenance forward by 3–6 months for two categories: (1) efficient "keep" fleet; (2) older aircraft slated for retirement/early lessor return

Digital-Twin Predictive Maintenance — Rising Demand

  • Lufthansa Technik: 15–20% increase in bookings for digital-twin-enabled predictive programs
  • MTU Aero Engines: 15–20% increase in bookings for digital-twin-enabled predictive programs
  • Real-time sensor analytics enabling targeted interventions during line maintenance instead of waiting for heavy checks

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Jet Fuel — The Defining MRO Challenge of 2026

  • American Airlines: >$4B additional 2026 fuel cost warning (April 23)
  • Delta Air Lines: ~$2B incremental Q2 2026 fuel cost
  • United Airlines: 5% summer capacity cut; 5 fare increases in 2026; checked-bag fees raised $10
  • Lufthansa Group: 20,000 short-haul flights removed; 27 aircraft grounded early
  • SAS: 1,000 flights cancelled in April
  • MRO solution: Accelerating heavy checks for efficient aircraft; installing fuel-saving modifications during planned downtime; retiring pre-2015 narrowbodies (15–22% higher fuel burn per seat)

Challenge 2: Fleet Retirement Acceleration — Dual-Edged Sword

  • Pre-2015 narrowbodies (737-800s, A320ceos) being retired early — 15–22% higher fuel burn per seat vs. newer models
  • Lessor data: 25–30% increase in early-return negotiations since March 2026
  • Early returns include pre-agreed heavy-check packages partly funded by lessees to speed re-marketing
  • MRO impact: Short-term boost from heavy-check packages for early-returned aircraft; BUT compressed long-term visibility for legacy narrowbody fleets

Challenge 3: Next-Generation Engine Durability Concerns

  • 66% of operators report next-gen narrow-body engine maintenance costs exceeding expectations by >21%
  • 25% report costs exceeding expectations by >50% (Oliver Wyman 2026 Survey)
  • GTF durability recovery: Ongoing but not yet complete
  • LEAP durability concerns: Emerging as strain shifts from GTF; GE/Safran raised shop-visit forecasts through 2028
