Market Updates and Growth Trends

Oliver Wyman 2026 MRO Survey: Industry at $136B and Counting Oliver Wyman

  • 2025 global MRO market size: >$136 billion (8% YoY growth over 2024)
  • Projected MRO spending by 2030: ~$193 billion — nearly double 2019 levels
  • Top 5 industry disruptors (next 5 years), per >150 aviation professional respondents:

1. Material and labor shortages + cost management (dominant for all surveyed years)

2. Geopolitical instability/tariffs (new 2026 category, instantly #2)

3–4. Additional disruptors ranked in top 4 (supply chain normalization delays, technology adoption gaps)

5. Generative AI adoption (first time in top 5)

  • Survey demographics: ~60% C-suite/VP respondents; 80% director-level or above; 70% with primary operations outside North America

European Jet Fuel Supply Crisis Deepens: Bidding War for Every Cargo

  • Strait of Hormuz closure (since Feb 28, 2026) has cut off 360,000 bpd of jet fuel — 20% of global shipped flows
  • Europe's daily jet fuel demand: ~1.6 million bpd; domestic production covers only 1.1 million bpd (~69%)
  • Import deficit: ~500,000 bpd required; traditional Middle East supply (75% of imports, ~375,000 bpd) largely dried up
  • Post-closure Middle East supply shortfall: ~175,000 bpd (53% of normal flows)
  • U.S. jet fuel exports hit record 442,000 bpd in early April 2026 (4-week avg 372,000 bpd, vs. 5-year norm of 172,000 bpd)
  • EU jet fuel supply estimate: ~5 months (Dutch government), as domestic production + strategic reserves buffer the shortfall
  • Societe Generale: Physical fuel scarcity is "existential" — "Paying more for energy is manageable; not having it is existential."

Supply Chain Developments

Engine MRO: Narrowbody Engine TATs Remain Elevated, Costs Exceed Expectations

  • Narrowbody engine turnaround times (TATs): Regularly 180–200+ days — well above pre-pandemic levels
  • TAT improvement outlook: >50% of Oliver Wyman survey respondents do not expect TAT improvements within the next 3 years (or ever)
  • Next-gen narrowbody engine shop costs:
  • 2/3 of respondents report costs exceed expectations by ≥21%
  • 1/4 of respondents report costs exceed expectations by ≥50%
  • Engine MRO accounts for >50% of total MRO spend globally

Material Cost Inflation Outpaces Expectations

  • 2025 material cost inflation outpaced expectations by 100–200 basis points per segment
  • 2026 outlook: Respondents expect material cost inflation to be higher again in 2026
  • Tariff impacts: 90% of respondents report experiencing tariff effects, specifically for component purchases, repairs, and engine overhauls
  • Supply chain normalization: Growing share of respondents believe post-COVID supply chain challenges will subside "much further in the future or never" — indicating a structural shift to a new baseline of higher costs and permanent scarcity

Pratt & Whitney GTF Advantage Engine Fully Certified for A320neo Family Aviation Week

  • FAA certification: February 2025; EASA validation: October 2025; EASA full type certification: April 2026
  • Key improvements over earlier GTF models:
  • 4–8% more takeoff thrust — enabling higher payload and longer range
  • Up to 2× time on wing — enhanced hot section durability, longer maintenance intervals
  • Fully interchangeable with current PW1100G-JM GTF models
  • HS+ retrofit option: Delivers 90–95% of full GTF Advantage durability benefits for existing fleet operators during routine shop visits
  • Entry into service: 2026; production standard target ~2028
  • P&W manufacturing investment: ~$1 billion for turbine airfoil facility in North Carolina; $200 million for forging capability in Georgia
  • GTF platform totals: 2,700+ aircraft delivered; 13,000+ engine orders and commitments across all variants

Technology and Investment News

AI in MRO: 58% Stuck at "Experimental" Stage, But Value Realization Improving

  • 58% of respondents (both 2025 and 2026 surveys) report their company is stuck at the "experimental" stage of AI development (stalled at proof-of-concept)
  • Value realization: 2/3 of respondents report realizing value from AI efforts as expected or higher — a strong uptick over prior years

  • Investment plans (next 5 years): 50% plan to invest in AI, but only 33% plan to invest in underlying IT infrastructure — a potential gap

Curtiss-Wright Switzerland Achieves ISO 14001 and ISO 45001 Certifications (April 28)

  • Facility operations: Motion control and stabilization systems; engineering, program management, and support services for defense and industrial markets

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: European Jet Fuel Supply — Physical Scarcity vs. Price

  • Root cause: Strait of Hormuz closure since Feb 28, 2026, cutting off 360,000 bpd of jet fuel flow
  • Europe's import dependency: 500,000 bpd required; Middle East traditionally supplied 75% (~375,000 bpd)
  • Alternative supply: U.S. exports surged to ~200,000 bpd to Europe (from pre-war 30,000–60,000 bpd), but Europe must "fight for every cargo" against Asian and U.S. domestic buyers
  • Airline impacts:
  • Lufthansa: Cancelled ~20,000 flights; saved >40,000 metric tons of jet fuel; 80% of 2026 fuel hedged
  • Air France-KLM: Fare hikes — +€100 long-haul return Economy; +€70 U.S./Canada/Mexico; +€10 short/medium-haul
  • IAG (BA, Aer Lingus, Iberia): No supply interruptions yet; requesting government slot alleviation flexibility
  • Wizz Air: Planning 17% capacity growth for summer 2026; 70% summer fuel hedged
  • EasyJet: No supply disruption; no surcharges on pre-booked flights or summer 2026 bookings

Challenge 2: Labor Market — Technician Shortages and Rising Attrition

  • Labor rate inflation: Pre-pandemic average ~3.0%; 2025 rate 5.5–6.0% across most categories
  • Attrition: Generally same or slightly worse than 2024; >50% of respondents expect attrition to worsen in 2026
  • Technician availability: 2/3 of respondents report finding qualified aircraft technicians/mechanics is moderately to very challenging
  • U.S. certified mechanics are not keeping pace with demand (per ATEC Pipeline Report)

Challenge 3: MRO Demand Bifurcation Under Fuel Crisis

  • Declining segments: Line maintenance (lower utilization from flight cuts), routine engine shop visits, legacy-engine shop visits
  • Rising segments: Heavy checks pulled forward for efficient aircraft, engine performance restoration, SAF compatibility testing, digital-twin predictive maintenance
  • Net impact: Global MRO demand trimmed 4–7% from base case in 2026; line maintenance and legacy-engine shop visits see steepest near-term contraction
  • Fleet retirement acceleration: Pre-2015 narrowbodies burning 15–22% more fuel per seat being retired early
