Market Updates & Growth Trends

  • MRO Greater China Day 2 (May 27) continued with significant partnership announcements and industry dialogue on supply chain localization, digital MRO, and workforce development in the Asia-Pacific region.
  • Reynolds Technical Services (Cincinnati, Ohio) obtained FAA Part 145 repair station certification (May 27), enabling the company to support public and third-party aircraft operators for the first time. Previously, Reynolds only serviced Reynolds Jet's internal corporate fleet.
  • Asia Digital Engineering (ADE) continued to ride momentum from its $100M QNB financing (announced May 22), with the company outlining expansion plans across line maintenance, base maintenance, and digital MRO at MRO Greater China.
2
🌏

Regional Expansion Activities

  • MTU Maintenance re-entered the Taiwanese market with a major win: EVA Air awarded MTU a long-term engine MRO contract covering CFM56-5B engines powering EVA's fleet of 17 Airbus A321-200 aircraft (May 27). All shop visits will be performed at MTU Maintenance Zhuhai (China) — a strategically significant location for serving the Asia-Pacific market.
  • SMBC Aviation Capital reported growth following completion of its Sumisho Air Lease Corporation acquisition (May 27). The Dublin-based combined entity now manages approximately 1,700 aircraft, making it one of the world's largest aircraft lessors — with significant implications for MRO contract placement globally.
  • Reynolds Technical Services FAA Part 145: The certification transforms Reynolds from a captive MRO into a commercial provider, adding capacity to the US corporate and regional aircraft MRO market from its Cincinnati base.
3
🔗

Supply Chain Developments

  • MTU-EVA Air CFM56-5B contract: The deal is significant for several reasons — it re-establishes MTU in Taiwan, utilizes MTU's Zhuhai facility (reinforcing China as an engine MRO hub), and secures long-term shop visit volume for the CFM56-5B, an engine type that will remain in service for another 15–20 years on the A321ceo fleet.
  • SMBC Aviation Capital scale: With ~1,700 aircraft under management, SMBC's MRO contract placement decisions have outsized influence on the global aftermarket. The lessor's growth means more transition checks, redelivery maintenance, and component support contracts flowing through the MRO ecosystem.
  • MRO Greater China workforce theme: Day 2 discussions highlighted China's growing pool of aviation maintenance technicians and engineers, positioning the country as both a market and a talent source for global MRO.
