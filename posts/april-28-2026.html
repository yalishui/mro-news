Airbus Q1 2026 Earnings: Sharp Profit Decline Amid Engine Shortages

  • Group revenue Q1 2026: €12.7B (down 7% YoY from Q1 2025)
  • EBIT Adjusted Q1 2026: €300M (down 52% YoY)
  • Commercial Aircraft EBIT Adjusted Q1 2026: €81M (down ~84% YoY)
  • Q1 2026 deliveries: 114 aircraft (down from 136 in Q1 2025, a 16.2% decline)
  • Drivers: Pratt & Whitney GTF engine shortage (dominant industrial issue), inventory buildup, unfavorable USD/EUR hedge rate
  • Full-year 2026 guidance maintained: ~870 commercial deliveries, ~€7.5B EBIT Adjusted, ~€4.5B free cash flow
  • Backlog: 9,037 aircraft (end-March 2026), up from 8,754 at end-2025
  • A320 production target: 70–75/month by end of 2027; stabilized at 75/month after 2027

Pratt & Whitney GTF Engine Crisis Remains "Dominant Industrial Issue" for Airbus Aviation Week

  • GTF engine delivery shortfall continues to be the primary constraint on Airbus commercial aircraft output
  • Parked, engineless A320neo family airframes accumulating outside final assembly lines — a "recurring feature" throughout 2024 and 2025

Engine MRO Capacity Allocation Shift

  • Independent MROs benefiting from engine shop capacity constraints; Sanad (UAE) achieved record 2025 revenue driven by global engine shop capacity constraints

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Airbus Profit Collapse — Supply Chain vs. Demand

  • Q1 2026 EBIT Adjusted collapse: Commercial aircraft EBIT Adjusted fell 84% YoY to just €81M
  • Root cause: GTF engine shortage preventing A320neo family delivery ramp-up; inventory buildup tying up cash
  • Airbus response: Maintaining full-year guidance (870 deliveries); back-loaded delivery pattern continues (same as 2025)
  • A220 constraints: Paced by Spirit AeroSystems work package integration (Airbus completed Spirit A220/A350 asset takeover in December 2025; €32M negative adjustment in Q1 accounts)

Challenge 2: Airline Fuel Cost Shock — MRO as Efficiency Lever

  • Fuel cost as % of operating expenses: Jumped from 25–30% historical norm to dominant expense line
  • Airline responses:
  • United: 5% summer capacity cut
  • Lufthansa Group: Removing 20,000 short-haul flights through October 2026; grounding 27 CityLine aircraft early
  • SAS: Cancelled ~1,000 April 2026 flights
  • MRO opportunity: Airlines pulling maintenance forward by 3–6 months for efficient aircraft; installing fuel-saving modifications (engine performance restoration, drag-reduction kits, deep engine washes)
  • Fleet retirement acceleration: Pre-2015 narrowbodies (737-800s, A320ceos) burning 15–22% more fuel per seat being retired early

Challenge 3: MRO Demand Bifurcation

  • Declining segments: Line maintenance (lower utilization), routine engine shop visits, legacy-engine shop visits
  • Rising segments: Heavy checks pulled forward, engine performance restoration, SAF compatibility testing, digital-twin predictive maintenance
  • Net impact: Global MRO demand trimmed 4–7% from base case in 2026; line maintenance and legacy-engine shop visits see steepest near-term contraction
