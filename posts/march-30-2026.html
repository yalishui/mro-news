Market Updates and Growth Trends

IBA: Engine Shortages and MRO Backlog Keep Values and Lease Rates Elevated

  • IBA Group (aviation market intelligence and consulting) published a detailed market update on March 24 confirming that persistent MRO backlogs and supply chain constraints continue to keep aircraft engine values and lease rates at elevated levels across all segments
  • Narrowbody engines:
  • CFM56-5B and CFM56-7B values have stabilised after ~18 months of increases, suggesting a plateau — but no near-term correction expected
  • V2500-A5 demand remains strong; traded values between $11M–$13M
  • LEAP and PW1100G (next-gen narrowbody) still facing short time-on-wing challenges driving persistently high AOG counts and sustained demand for spare engines
  • IBA flagged upcoming CFM LEAP Advantage and Hot Section Plus (HS+) upgrade programmes as future value catalysts
  • Widebody engines:
  • Boeing 777-300ER engine full-life values have risen significantly since 2023, now exceeding $32M; short-term lease rates at $170,000–$185,000/month
  • Trent 700 (A330ceo) post-overhaul values rising sharply — half-life values tracking upward with the broader market
  • Regional/turboprop engines:
  • CF34-8E values supported by North American scope clauses limiting E175-E2 deliveries
  • PW127M (ATR) values under upward pressure due to key component shortages
  • IBA outlook: Demand for serviceable engines continues to exceed supply; MRO demand, supply chain constraints, and aircraft delivery delays will remain key value drivers throughout H1 2026

Source: IBA Group, March 24, 2026

Regional Expansion Activities

Lufthansa Technik LTCS Opens 25,000 sq ft Tulsa Facility — Americas MRO Hub Milestone LHT

  • Lufthansa Technik Component Services (LTCS) officially opened a new 25,000 sq ft state-of-the-art facility at Tulsa International Airport, Oklahoma on March 26–27 — its third building at the campus and the first major milestone in a two-phase expansion plan
  • New capabilities added:
  • 90 new workstations across upgraded avionics and component repair shops
  • New Integrated Drive Generator (IDG) repair and overhaul capability for Airbus A320ceo/neo and Boeing 737NG/MAX aircraft — a strategically important addition given the high cycle volumes of these fleets
  • Expanded administrative and logistics areas integrated with existing hydraulics, pneumatics, fuel systems, emergency equipment, and galley component shops
  • Phase 2 planned: LTCS has announced intent to more than double the new building's footprint, adding capacity in pneumatics and complex avionics
  • Strategic context: Tulsa serves as LTCS's Americas gateway, integrated into Lufthansa Technik's global network (Hamburg, Frankfurt, Shenzhen); 24/7 parts availability supported by regional strategic inventory positions
  • Official statements:
  • LTCS MD Tobias Baumgart: "This investment reflects our 'think local, act local' strategy — we are here for the long term."
  • LHT COO Harald Gloy: "The Americas is a key market; this expansion reaffirms our commitment to world-class technical service."
  • Oklahoma Commerce CEO John Budd: "This cements Oklahoma's position as a leading Americas aerospace MRO hub."

Source: Lufthansa Technik press release, March 26, 2026; Tulsa Chamber, AirwaysMag, AviTrader, March 27–28, 2026

Philippine Airlines and AFI KLM E&M Renew Long-Term GE90 Engine Partnership AFI KLM E&M

  • Philippine Airlines (PAL) and Air France Industries KLM Engineering & Maintenance (AFI KLM E&M) announced the renewal and extension of their long-term maintenance agreement covering PAL's GE90 engines (powering its Boeing 777 widebody fleet)
  • Agreement scope:
  • Full shop visit maintenance (overhaul), predictive maintenance, and spare parts support
  • Multi-year term extension reflecting PAL's continued long-haul fleet strategy
  • Builds on an already established partnership as PAL's 777 fleet enters a more mature phase of its lifecycle
  • Strategic significance: GE90 is the most powerful commercial aircraft engine ever built; shop visits are complex, lengthy, and expensive — long-term MRO partnerships provide cost predictability and slot security in a constrained overhaul market
  • Market context: Engine MRO capacity for large widebody powerplants remains particularly tight; securing dedicated shop visit slots via multi-year agreements is a competitive priority for carriers operating 777-200ER/300ER fleets

Source: AFI KLM E&M press release / Aviation News Online / Centre for Aviation, March 26–27, 2026

Fleet Renewal and Engine MRO Implications

Finnair Orders Up to 46 Embraer E195-E2s with Pratt & Whitney Engine Support Package Aviation Week

  • Finnair announced a major fleet renewal order for up to 46 Embraer E195-E2 regional jets (30 firm + 16 options + 12 purchase rights), announced March 23 — described by CEO Turkka Kuusisto as "one of the largest investments in Finnair's 102-year history"
  • MRO implications:
  • The order includes a comprehensive long-term engine support agreement with Pratt & Whitney (RTX) covering spare engines and maintenance services for the PW1900G Geared Turbofan (GTF) engines
  • The E195-E2 will replace Finnair's older narrowbody fleet (Airbus A320/321ceo aircraft also being retired), simplifying the maintenance base and transitioning to a single GTF powerplant type for regional operations
  • P&W GTF support infrastructure in Europe is well-established, with existing MRO agreements across Lufthansa Technik, MTU Maintenance, and other network shops
  • Fleet transition context: Finnair is simultaneously phasing out A320/321ceo aircraft — creating a near-term maintenance wind-down opportunity for legacy CFM56 and IAE V2500 shops currently supporting those types
  • Regional market signal: The E195-E2's strong order momentum in 2025–2026 (also selected by airlines across Europe and Latin America) continues to drive demand for PW1900G MRO capacity build-out

Source: Embraer, AeroTime, Aviation Analysis, MRO Business Today, March 23–25, 2026

Industry Outlook

Entering Q2 2026: The final stretch to MRO Americas is now in motion. This week marks the beginning of peak deal-announcement season as companies position their news flow ahead of Orlando. The LTCS Tulsa opening and the Philippine Airlines/AFI KLM GE90 renewal both underscore the same structural theme: in a market where MRO capacity is structurally constrained across all aircraft types, long-term partnerships and physical infrastructure investment are the primary competitive moats.

The IBA engine values report reinforces that the fundamental supply-demand imbalance — too many aircraft needing shop visits, too few certified slots available — will persist through at least H2 2026. Airlines that locked in multi-year engine MRO agreements in 2024–2025 are now seeing tangible competitive advantages in operational reliability and cost predictability versus those navigating the spot market.

With MRO Americas three weeks out, the industry enters its highest-visibility period of the year. Expect the pace of announcements to intensify daily through April 20.
