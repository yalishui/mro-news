Market Updates and Growth Trends

Oliver Wyman: MRO Market Surpasses $136B in 2025; Supply Chain Disruptors Reshape Industry Oliver Wyman

  • 2025 global MRO market: $136B (+8% YoY) — Oliver Wyman 2026 MRO Survey (150+ aviation professionals, 60% senior executives, 70% outside North America)
  • 2036 projection: Approaching $193B (nearly double 2019 levels)
  • Top 5 industry disruptors (next 5 years):

1. Material and labor shortages/cost management — continues to dominate

2. Geopolitical instability/tariffs — new category that instantly rose to #2

3. Generative AI adoption — rounded out top five

  • Material cost inflation exceeded forecasts by 100–200 basis points in 2025
  • 90% of respondents report experiencing tariff impacts (especially component purchases, repairs, and engine overhauls)
  • Industry increasingly believes supply chain challenges will never fully subside — structural shift to a new higher-cost baseline

Engine MRO Remains the Dominant Spend Category

  • Engine MRO: Accounts for more than 50% of total MRO spend
  • Narrowbody engine TATs: Now regularly 180–200+ days (well above pre-pandemic levels)
  • >50% of respondents do not expect TAT improvement within the next 3 years (if ever)
  • Two-thirds of respondents report shop costs for next-gen narrowbody engines (GTF, LEAP) exceeding expectations by 21% or more; 25% report cost overruns of 50%+

North American MRO: Engine Maintenance to Drive 44% Growth by 2035

  • Aviation Week Intelligence Network (AWIN) forecast: Engine maintenance spending in North America will rise 44% by 2035 to $19.4B

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Supply Chain — "New Normal" Requires Structural Adaptation

  • 90% of MRO operators affected by tariffs; material cost inflation exceeding forecasts by 100–200 bps
  • Supply chain challenges deemed permanent — industry must adapt structurally, not wait for normalization
  • Solution: Strategic inventory buildup; diversification of supplier base; greater investment in data infrastructure

Challenge 2: Labor — Chronic Shortage with High Attrition

  • Labor rate inflation: 5.5–6.0% across most categories (vs. ~3.0% pre-pandemic)
  • Stubbornly high for engines and components specifically
  • Two-thirds of respondents find it moderately to very challenging to find aircraft technicians/mechanics
  • >50% expect attrition to worsen next year — US certified mechanics not keeping pace with demand
  • Solutions: Earlier education pipeline (AMT donations, apprenticeships), developmental training programs, improved benefits

Challenge 3: AI Integration — Experimental Stage vs. Value Expectation Gap

  • Despite strong AI experimentation results, 58% still stuck at experimental — infrastructure gap
  • Solution: Invest in IT infrastructure alongside AI; data quality improvement as prerequisite
  • Half of companies planning AI investment (next 5 years) but only one-third investing in IT infrastructure — disconnect needs resolution

Challenge 4: Next-Gen Engine Cost Overruns — Systemic Issue

  • Two-thirds report next-gen narrowbody engine (GTF, LEAP) shop costs exceeding expectations by 21%+; 25% exceed by 50%+
  • TATs of 180–200+ days now normal — no relief expected for 3+ years per majority of operators
  • Solutions: Fleet planning adjustments; predictive maintenance to pre-empt failures; OEM-MRO collaboration on shop process optimization

Challenge 5: Integration Risk — Mega M&A Execution

  • VSE–PAG ($2.025B) and AAR restructuring represent two of the largest MRO platform changes of 2026
  • Integration execution risk significant: cross-selling, repair insourcing, procurement efficiencies are key synergy levers
  • VSE expects clear path to >20% Adjusted EBITDA margins through disciplined integration
