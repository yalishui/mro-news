Market Updates and Growth Trends

AAR CORP. Announces Major Segment Realignment — Legacy Commercial Programs to Wind Down (May 6)

  • Announcement date: May 6, 2026
  • Effective: Q4 FY2026 (beginning approximately August 1, 2026)
  • New four-segment structure:

1. Parts Supply — new parts distribution and used serviceable material (USM)

2. Repair, Engineering, and Software — MRO services (Airframe MRO, Component MRO), plus Trax℠, Aerostrat℠, and Airvoyant℠ software platforms

3. Government Solutions — fleet management, customer-owned aircraft operations, performance-based logistics programs, and Mobility Systems (formerly Expeditionary Services)

4. Legacy Commercial Programs — asset-heavy flight hour-based component repair programs for commercial airlines (being wound down)

  • Legacy Commercial Programs financials (LTM to Feb 28, 2026):
  • Sales: $252.4M
  • GAAP operating loss: $(0.2)M
  • Adjusted operating income: $5.0M
  • Net assets (as of Feb 28, 2026): ~$160M
  • Wind-down rationale: Segment "requires significant asset pools and no longer meets our capital return thresholds"
  • Timeline: Approximately 3–4 years to complete wind-down
  • Expected outcomes: Periodic gains anticipated as assets divested; talented team members to be redeployed to other AAR growth initiatives
  • CEO John M. Holmes: "Once complete, we believe the wind-down of Legacy Commercial Programs will result in a more simplified business model with higher margins and improved returns on capital."
  • Guidance unchanged: Q4 FY2026 and full-year FY2026 guidance (ending May 31, 2026) unaffected

MRO Americas 2026: 30th Anniversary Wrap — AI, GTF/LEAP, and Workforce Dominate (Orlando, April 21–23)

Duncan Aviation Donates Honeywell TFE731 Engine to Lincoln North Star High School AMT Program

  • Company: Duncan Aviation
  • Recipient: Lincoln North Star High School aviation program (Lincoln, Nebraska)
  • Donated engine: Honeywell TFE731 (originally earmarked for Duncan Aviation's apprentice program in Provo, Utah; reallocated to avoid duplicating resources and maximize community impact)
  • Key quote — Kasey Harwick, Executive VP & COO: "We've always believed in using real-world equipment to build confidence and competence in our technicians. When this engine no longer had a home internally, it made perfect sense to extend that same opportunity to students at Lincoln North Star."
  • Significance: Second major AMT workforce pipeline investment in two days (following GA Telesis $4.1M donation on May 5)

Major Challenges and Solutions

Challenge: Legacy Business Divestment Complexity

  • AAR's Legacy Commercial Programs involves long-term airline contracts, customer relationships, and significant asset pools
  • Solution: 3–4 year wind-down timeline allows orderly contract completion and asset monetization; team redeployment preserves institutional knowledge

Challenge: MRO Labor Pipeline — Short-Term vs. Long-Term

  • Two major engine donations in consecutive days (GA Telesis $4.1M on May 5; Duncan Aviation TFE731 on May 6) highlight industry-wide AMT shortage
  • Short-term solutions: Engine donations, apprenticeships, high school programs
  • Long-term challenge: US certified mechanics not keeping pace with demand; >50% of Oliver Wyman survey respondents expect attrition to worsen
